## Deal Qualification And Risk

- **Risk – Economic buyer absent:** CFO not engaged on a $180k ARR deal late in Evaluation. Without CFO sponsorship, close date is unrealistic.
- **Risk – Security gate first:** HIPAA audit-evidence concern is on the critical path; failing security review halts legal.
- **Risk – Single-threaded champion:** Maya is the only named advocate. Champion loss = deal loss.
- **Risk – Incumbent advantage:** Switching cost, data migration, and clinician change-management favor the incumbent unless differentiation is sharp.
- **Risk – Close-date realism:** With security review + legal still ahead and CFO not engaged, 2026-06-30 is plausible only if security package lands in the next sprint and CFO is engaged immediately. `[UNKNOWN]` exact remaining calendar.
- **Risk – Decision criteria not documented:** Without written criteria, the incumbent can move the goalposts.

## Closing Strategy (Stage: Evaluation)

Goal of this stage: convert evaluation into a written, mutually agreed close plan with CFO engaged and security cleared.

1. **Lock decision criteria in writing** with Maya — including HIPAA audit-evidence acceptance criteria from security.
2. **Trigger security review now** — deliver compliance package and offer a live security working session.
3. **Engineer CFO entry** — co-build a one-page business case with Maya; request a 30-minute CFO meeting.
4. **Multi-thread** — earn a second advocate (IT Director or compliance lead).
5. **Pre-stage legal** — share standard MSA/BAA with procurement contact `[UNKNOWN]` so legal isn't a cold start after security.
6. **Build urgency honestly** — anchor to a real Northstar event (compliance, fiscal, incumbent renewal) rather than artificial pressure.
