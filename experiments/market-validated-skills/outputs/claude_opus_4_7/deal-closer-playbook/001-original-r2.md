## 4. Objection Playbook

### KNOWN OBJECTION: "Our security team is worried about HIPAA audit evidence."

**Why they say this:** The security team has likely been burned before — either an audit finding, a near-miss, or a peer institution's incident. They need to know that if an OCR auditor walks in tomorrow, they can pull a clean evidence package from your platform. This is a defensibility concern, not a feature concern.

**Response Framework:**
1. **Acknowledge:** "Audit defensibility is exactly the right thing for your security team to be focused on — it is what we get asked about most by clinic networks."
2. **Reframe:** Shift from "do you have security features" to "can you produce evidence on demand."
3. **Evidence:** Walk through your audit-evidence package — access logs, message audit trail, BAA, encryption attestations, SOC 2 / HITRUST report if applicable, breach notification SLAs.
4. **Confirm:** "If we walk your security team through a sample audit-evidence pull next week, would that resolve the concern, or are there other specific scenarios they want to test?"

**Example response:**
> "Maya, I want to take this one seriously because audit evidence is where most messaging platforms fall short — they have the controls but cannot produce the artifacts. Here is what I would like to do: get your security lead on a 45-minute call with our compliance team. We will walk through a live audit-evidence pull for a sample patient interaction — every access event, every message, every consent record, exportable in the format OCR expects. If that does not resolve the concern, I want to know exactly what would. Can we get that on the calendar this week?"

**If they push back** (e.g., "we still are not sure"): Offer a written audit-evidence sample document they can review asynchronously, and offer to put the security lead in touch with a reference customer's security lead.

- **Acknowledge:** Switching cost is real.
