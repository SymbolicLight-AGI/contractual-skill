## Deal Qualification And Risk

### Qualification Assessment
- **Need:** Likely strong – Maya Chen is championing a secure messaging platform, suggesting a real operational gap vs. the incumbent portal.
- **Budget:** Unconfirmed. CFO not engaged; $180k ARR may require new budget line or reallocation.
- **Authority:** Economic buyer (CFO) not engaged; champion lacks final sign-off.
- **Timeline:** Target close 2026-06-30 is ~3 months away from now (prompt date not specified, assume today is early 2026). Closing by end of Q2 is plausible but aggressive given security review and lack of CFO engagement.

### Key Risks
1. **[Risk – Blocker Influence]** The security team’s HIPAA audit evidence objection could delay or derail the deal if not resolved with concrete product evidence, references, and a security architecture review.
2. **[Risk – Economic Buyer Absence]** Without the CFO’s alignment, the deal may stall at procurement or fail to get budget approval. Competitor can undermine by framing our pricing as risky switch.
3. **[Risk – Competitor Incumbency]** The incumbent patient portal vendor has established relationships, integration, and switching costs. If the portal already includes basic messaging, Maya must justify a differentiated secure messaging platform.
4. **[Risk – Procurement Process]** Security review must come before legal. If security approval delays, legal and contract negotiation push the close date beyond Q2, potentially into Q3.
5. **[Risk – Close-Date Realism]** Evaluation stage in April 2026? With unengaged CFO, security objection, and no identified user buyers, a June 30 close is optimistic. A mutual close plan must compress steps without skipping necessary gates.
6. **[Risk – Missing Decision Criteria]** We do not know how Northstar will measure success (e.g., must lower no-show rates, improve patient satisfaction scores, reduce administrative call volume). Without this, value prop is generic.

## Competitive Positioning

**Competitor:** Incumbent patient portal vendor.

**Positioning stance:**
- **[Inference]** The incumbent offers a general patient portal which may include basic secure messaging but is likely not a dedicated engagement platform. Our product should be positioned as a specialized, mobile-first, secure communication layer that complements or replaces the portal’s messaging component.
- **[Fact]** Our product’s HIPAA audit trail capability must be highlighted as a differentiator if the portal lacks granular logging.
- **[Action]** Build a “Why Better than the Portal” one-pager covering:
  - Real-time two-way messaging vs. asynchronous portal inbox.
  - Dedicated audit logs per message thread, exportable for compliance.
  - Better user adoption (patients prefer SMS/app-like experience).
  - Lower administrative overhead and faster response cycles.
- **[Risk]** Incumbent vendor may bundle messaging as a free add-on or reduce price to retain account. Monitor for aggressive retention tactics. Prepare a TCO comparison showing long-term value against hidden costs of poor patient communication.
- **[Evidence Gap]** No specific intel on competitor’s current messaging capabilities or contract end date. This must be gathered via Maya or coach.

## Closing Strategy

**Given Stage: Evaluation**  
The goal is to move from Evaluation → Business Case Creation → Security Approval → Legal/Procurement → Negotiation → Close by June 30, 2026.

**Strategic Moves:**

1. **Immediate: Unlock the Economic Buyer gate.**  
   - Help Maya craft an internal business case and schedule a joint meeting with the CFO to present ROI, risk of status quo, and implementation timeline. Include a cost avoidance argument (e.g., potential HIPAA breach fines due to non-compliant texting).  
   - **[Action]** Sales must prepare an executive summary deck tailored to the CFO, focusing on financial impact, not features.

2. **Resolve Security Objection First – Front-load Technical Validation.**  
   - Arrange a dedicated security deep-dive with Security team and Director of IT. Present the audit evidence pack, BAA template, encryption details, and incident response plan.  
   - **[Action]** Get the security team to sign off on the architecture, or at least map out remaining concerns, so that the formal security review is a formality, not a blocker.

3. **Engage Director of IT for Technical Win.**  
   - Conduct a technical evaluation covering integration, scalability, identity management, and data residency. Propose a sandbox or proof of concept if feasible.  
   - **[Action]** Deliver a customized technical architecture document. Secure IT’s endorsement before legal review begins.

4. **Identify and Cultivate a Coach.**  
   - Ask Maya if there is a supportive colleague in IT, compliance, or a physician leader who can provide internal insight and advocate. This will be critical for navigating procurement and blocking the incumbent’s influence.

5. **Mitigate Incumbent Threat.**  
   - Uncover the incumbent’s contract end date and pain points. If the portal is up for renewal soon, align our timeline to that forcing function. If not, build a parallel track where messaging platform can co-exist initially with a planned migration.

6. **Align on Mutual Close Plan (see below).**
