## Closing Strategy

The immediate focus is to unblock procurement and security; otherwise, the deal cannot advance to negotiation or close.

**Phase 1 – Unblock (Current -> 2–3 weeks)**
- Reduce unknowns: Identify the procurement and security stakeholders (Sam can introduce). Gather exact documentation requirements.
- Internal handoff to our security/compliance team to prepare SOC 2 evidence package (or equivalent) and data residency statement.
- Internal handoff to legal/procurement side to initiate vendor registration forms and review standard bank terms (if they will be provided).
- No customer‑facing communication until internal materials are ready and approved by respective teams.

**Phase 2 – Evaluation completion (3–5 weeks)**
- Submit procurement package and security documentation once approved internally.
- Track review timelines; schedule a follow‑up call with Sam and the security/procurement contacts to address questions.
- Parallel: Continue building COO relationship through Sam, sharing progress on risk mitigation.

**Phase 3 – Negotiation & close (before 2026-07-15)**
- Once approval gates are passed, move to formal proposal refinement and contract negotiations with COO and legal.
- Use “unblocked” status as leverage to maintain urgency; emphasize that all pre‑conditions have been met.
