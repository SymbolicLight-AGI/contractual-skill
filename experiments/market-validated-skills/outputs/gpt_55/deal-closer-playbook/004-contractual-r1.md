## 8. Closing Strategy

### Strategic Objective

Move the deal from “proposal blocked by vendor/security review” to “approved vendor + cleared security path + final commercial/legal review.”

### Recommended Internal Strategy

1. **Create a single internal deal-risk owner**
   - **Action:** Assign AE as deal quarterback, with clear support from Security/GRC, Legal, Procurement/Deal Desk, and Solutions.
   - **Reason:** Procurement/security blockers require coordinated responses and controlled evidence sharing.

2. **Build an EastRiver vendor approval packet**
   - **Action:** Prepare standard vendor documentation before asking Sam/procurement for forms.
   - **Reason:** Reduces cycle time once procurement confirms requirements.

3. **Confirm SOC 2 sharing process**
   - **Action:** Security/GRC to verify what SOC 2 evidence can be shared, whether NDA is required, and approved delivery method.
   - **Human gate:** Security/GRC approval required before sharing SOC 2 materials.

4. **Prepare data residency response**
   - **Action:** Solutions + Security + Legal prepare a concise, approved response covering data storage, processing, backups, access, and subprocessors.
   - **Human gate:** Do not make region-specific or contractual commitments without Legal/Security/Product approval.

5. **Map approval sequence**
   - **Action:** Through Sam, request internal process clarity: procurement owner, security owner, required docs, dates, and final approver.
   - **Note:** No customer-facing message should be sent yet. Draft internally first.

6. **Engage economic buyer only after blocker plan is credible**
   - **Action:** Prepare COO update talking points showing specific blockers, owners, dates, and asks.
   - **Reason:** Executive escalation works better when tied to a precise decision path.
