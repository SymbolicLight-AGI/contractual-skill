## 5. Deal Qualification And Risk

### Qualification Assessment

| Area | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Pain | Partially known. Need specifics on why secure messaging is being evaluated now. |
| Champion | Strong initial signal: Maya Chen is VP Operations and named champion. |
| Economic buyer | Weak: CFO not yet engaged. |
| Technical validation | In progress or pending; Director of IT identified. |
| Security | Active risk due to HIPAA audit evidence concern. |
| Competition | Incumbent patient portal vendor creates status quo risk. |
| Procurement/legal | Sequence known: security before legal. Details unknown. |
| Close date realism | At risk unless security review and CFO engagement happen quickly. |

### Key Risks

1. **Economic buyer not engaged**
   - **Risk:** CFO may reject, delay, or re-scope the purchase late in the cycle.
   - **Action:** Schedule CFO alignment meeting with Maya within the next week.

2. **Security review is gating legal**
   - **Risk:** HIPAA audit evidence concern could block legal review and compress the close timeline.
   - **Action:** Deliver security/HIPAA evidence package and hold a security Q&A session.

3. **Incumbent vendor advantage**
   - **Risk:** The incumbent may win by inertia, bundled pricing, existing workflows, or perceived lower switching cost.
   - **Action:** Build a side-by-side value comparison focused on secure messaging depth, workflow outcomes, compliance evidence, and operational impact.

4. **Unknown procurement/legal timelines**
   - **Risk:** Even if evaluation is successful, process steps may exceed the 2026-06-30 target.
   - **Action:** Get procurement checklist, security requirements, legal review SLA, and signature process immediately.

5. **Unclear business case**
   - **Risk:** $180k ARR requires CFO-level justification.
   - **Action:** Quantify operational impact: staff time saved, reduced call volume, faster patient communication, compliance assurance, and potential consolidation value.

## 8. Closing Strategy

### Primary Strategy

Move from evaluation to controlled close by resolving the two largest risks first:

1. **Security/HIPAA evidence concern**
2. **CFO economic buyer engagement**

### Stage-Based Strategy: Evaluation

At Evaluation stage, the goal is not to negotiate first. The goal is to create confidence, complete validation, and lock the buying process.

### Recommended Sequence

1. **Confirm decision criteria**
   - Ask Maya and Director of IT: “What must be true for Northstar to select a vendor?”
   - Include security, workflow, integration, budget, and user adoption criteria.

2. **Resolve security gate**
   - Get exact security artifact list.
   - Provide approved HIPAA/security documentation.
   - Schedule security review meeting with Director of IT and security stakeholders.

3. **Engage CFO**
   - Use Maya as sponsor.
   - Present business case and decision timeline.
   - Validate budget and approval process.

4. **Differentiate from incumbent**
   - Run focused comparison against current-state pain.
   - Make status quo cost visible.
   - Avoid unverified competitor claims.

5. **Build mutual close plan**
   - Work backward from 2026-06-30.
   - Assign owners and dates for security, legal, procurement, CFO approval, and signature.

6. **Prepare negotiation boundaries**
   - Identify likely commercial questions but do not approve discounts or terms.
   - Escalate any pricing, contract, or legal changes through proper human gates.
