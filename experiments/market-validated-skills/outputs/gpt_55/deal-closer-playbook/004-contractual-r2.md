## 6. Objection Playbook

### Objection 1: “We need SOC 2 evidence before vendor approval.”

**Response strategy, internal only:**

- **Fact:** Security has requested SOC 2 evidence.
- **Action:** Route request to internal security/compliance owner.
- **Action:** Provide only approved security materials, such as SOC 2 report, bridge letter, security overview, or trust package if available and approved.
- **Human gate:** Do not share SOC 2 or security documents externally until approved by security/legal/compliance process.

**Internal talk track for AE / deal team:**

> We need to confirm exactly what SOC 2 evidence EastRiver requires, who can access it, whether NDA is required, and whether a bridge letter or additional controls mapping is needed. Security/compliance should own the evidence package and approval to share.

---

### Objection 2: “Procurement needs vendor approval before we can proceed.”

**Response strategy, internal only:**

- **Fact:** Procurement vendor onboarding is a blocker.
- **Action:** Ask Sam, when approved to communicate externally, for the vendor onboarding checklist, procurement contact, required documentation, and target approval date.
- **Action:** Internally prepare standard vendor packet: W-9/tax forms, insurance certificates, financial information, security package, privacy documents, legal entity details, and accessibility documentation if applicable.
- **Human gate:** Procurement or legal must approve any vendor forms, certifications, or contract representations before submission.

**Internal talk track:**

> We should not wait for procurement to surface requirements one by one. We need the full vendor onboarding checklist and an owner for each required item.

---

### Objection 3: “Where will our data reside?”

**Response strategy, internal only:**

- **Fact:** Data residency questions are a blocker.
- **Action:** Identify what data EastRiver expects to process in the platform.
- **Action:** Confirm supported hosting regions, subprocessors, backup/replication regions, and any cross-border data transfer implications with security/privacy/product.
- **Human gate:** Do not commit to a specific data residency posture, region, architecture, or contractual data restriction without approval from security, legal, and product/engineering.

**Internal talk track:**

> We need to distinguish between actual product capability, contractual commitments, and customer preference. Data residency answers should be validated before being shared.

---

### Objection 4: “We cannot finalize until security and procurement are complete.”

**Response strategy, internal only:**

- **Inference:** This is likely a process blocker rather than a value objection.
- **Action:** Build a mutual close plan that runs security, procurement, legal, and commercial approvals in parallel.
- **Action:** Ask Sam to confirm internal sequencing and whether the COO can help unblock if timelines slip.
- **Human gate:** Any escalation to COO or customer executives requires sales manager approval.

## 8. Closing Strategy

### Primary Objective

Move the deal from **proposal stalled by risk review** to **parallel procurement/security/legal path with named owners, evidence requirements, and dates**.

### Strategy By Workstream

#### A. Security Workstream

**Owner:** Internal security/compliance lead  
**Goal:** Determine and deliver approved SOC 2/security evidence package.

Actions:

1. Confirm what SOC 2 evidence is available and shareable.
2. Determine whether NDA or controlled access is required.
3. Prepare approved security packet.
4. Identify owner for completing security questionnaire, if required.
5. Validate data residency response with security/product/legal.

#### B. Procurement Workstream

**Owner:** AE + internal deal desk / finance / legal as needed  
**Goal:** Complete vendor onboarding requirements without creating contract risk.

Actions:

1. Obtain procurement checklist and required forms.
2. Assign internal owners for each document.
3. Confirm vendor onboarding timeline and approval sequence.
4. Identify any required insurance, tax, financial, accessibility, compliance, or diversity documentation.
5. Track procurement status separately from legal contract status.

#### C. Legal / Privacy Workstream

**Owner:** Legal / privacy counsel  
**Goal:** Prepare for DPA/MSA/order form review and data residency language.

Actions:

1. Confirm whether EastRiver requires their paper or our paper.
2. Identify DPA/privacy review requirements.
3. Validate any data residency or subprocessors language.
4. Ensure no unsupported commitments are made in procurement questionnaires.

#### D. Executive / Business Workstream

**Owner:** AE + sales manager  
**Goal:** Keep COO-level business momentum while risk review proceeds.

Actions:

1. Confirm Sam’s ability to keep COO engaged.
2. Validate business impact, urgency, and approval deadline.
3. Prepare internal escalation path if security/procurement delays threaten target close date.
4. Do not escalate externally without sales manager approval.
