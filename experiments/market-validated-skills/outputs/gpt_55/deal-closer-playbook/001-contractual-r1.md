### Executive Readout

**Fact:** Northstar Clinics is evaluating a secure patient messaging platform with a target close date of **2026-06-30** and a deal size of **$180k ARR**.

**Primary deal risk:** The CFO is not yet engaged, while security review is a gating step before legal. **Recommended closing posture:** Treat this as a security-led evaluation with executive value validation. The team should not push straight to legal until security requirements, CFO approval path, and incumbent replacement risk are clearly addressed.

## 7. Competitive Positioning

### Competitive Situation

**Fact:** The competitor is the incumbent patient portal vendor.

### Likely Incumbent Advantages

| Advantage | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Existing relationship | Northstar may trust the known vendor |
| Existing integration | Switching may seem risky |
| User familiarity | Staff may resist new workflows |
| Procurement simplicity | Renewing may be easier than onboarding a new vendor |
| Perceived lower risk | Security and legal may already have approved incumbent |

### Positioning Strategy

Do not attack the incumbent generically. Instead, force a criteria-based comparison.

#### Recommended Positioning Frame

“Northstar should choose the option that best satisfies its secure patient messaging requirements, audit evidence expectations, operational workflows, and financial case. If the incumbent meets those standards, staying put may be rational. If there are gaps in secure messaging depth, staff workflow efficiency, patient engagement, or audit evidence, those gaps should drive the decision.”

### Competitive Discovery Questions

1. What does the incumbent patient portal do well today?
2. Where does it fall short for secure messaging?
3. Are patients using the current portal consistently?
4. Are staff managing messages outside the portal today?
5. Are there unresolved compliance, audit, or reporting concerns?
6. What would Northstar need to see to justify switching?
7. When does the incumbent agreement renew?
8. Is the incumbent quoting a renewal or expansion?
9. What decision criteria will be used to compare solutions?

### Competitive Positioning Matrix

| Decision Area | Our Positioning Approach | Evidence Needed |
|---|---|---|
| Secure messaging workflow | Emphasize purpose-built messaging and operational fit if supported by product facts | Product demo proof, workflow map |
| HIPAA audit evidence | Show structured security evidence package | Approved security documentation |
| Operational efficiency | Tie to VP Operations priorities | Current workflow pain and ROI model |
| IT confidence | Provide architecture and controls | Technical documentation |
| Financial approval | Build CFO business case | ROI, budget fit, cost of delay |
| Switching risk | Reduce uncertainty with implementation plan | Implementation plan approved by delivery/CS |
| Incumbent comparison | Use Northstar’s criteria, not generic claims | Confirmed decision criteria |

## 8. Closing Strategy

### Stage-Based Strategy: Evaluation

Because the deal is in **Evaluation**, the objective is not to negotiate first. The objective is to create consensus that:

1. The platform solves a high-priority operational and security-sensitive problem.
2. The security review can be completed successfully.
3. The CFO agrees the value justifies the $180k ARR investment.
4. The solution beats the incumbent on agreed decision criteria.
5. Procurement and legal can complete in time for 2026-06-30.

### Recommended Deal Strategy

#### 1. Convert Security Concern Into a Checklist

**Fact:** Security worries about HIPAA audit evidence.  
**Action:** Ask Northstar for a written security checklist or questionnaire.  
**Goal:** Prevent vague security concerns from becoming an open-ended blocker.

#### 2. Engage CFO Before Security Completion

**Fact:** CFO is not yet engaged.  
**Risk:** CFO approval may delay or reset the deal.  
**Action:** Ask Maya to sponsor a CFO meeting focused on business value, budget path, and decision process.

Suggested internal ask to Maya:

> “To protect the June 30 timeline, we should align with the CFO while security review is underway. Can we schedule a 30-minute value and approval-path discussion next week?”

#### 3. Create a Mutual Close Plan

**Fact:** Target close date is 2026-06-30.  
**Risk:** Security before legal creates a sequential process.  
**Action:** Propose a mutual plan with dated security, CFO, procurement, and legal milestones.

#### 4. Build Incumbent Replacement Case

**Fact:** Competitor is incumbent patient portal vendor.  
**Risk:** Status quo wins unless pain is specific and quantified.  
**Action:** Create a comparison against Northstar’s decision criteria and identify where the incumbent is insufficient.

#### 5. Prepare Parallel Internal Handoffs

**Fact:** Security review must happen before legal.  
**Action:** Internally prepare legal, security, procurement, and implementation materials now, while respecting customer process.

## 11. Negotiation Guidance

### Commercial Posture

**Do not lead with discounting.** The current issue is not stated as price; the known blocker is security evidence, and the major missing stakeholder is the CFO.

### Recommended Negotiation Sequence

1. Confirm pain and decision criteria.
2. Resolve security evidence concern.
3. Engage CFO on value and budget path.
4. Confirm incumbent comparison and switching rationale.
5. Only then discuss commercial structure if needed.

### Guardrails

| Area | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Discounting | Do not offer or imply discounts without sales leadership approval |
| Payment terms | Do not alter payment timing without finance/deal desk approval |
| Contract terms | Legal must approve any contract or BAA changes |
| Security commitments | Security/legal must approve compliance representations |
| Implementation | Delivery/CS must approve scope, timeline, and resource commitments |
| Outcomes | Do not guarantee patient engagement, cost savings, compliance status, or operational results |

### If CFO Pushes on Price

Recommended response:

> “I understand the need to evaluate cost carefully. Before discussing any commercial changes, let’s make sure we are aligned on the business value, risk reduction, and operational impact Northstar expects. If there is a commercial gap after that, I can take it through the appropriate internal approval process.”
