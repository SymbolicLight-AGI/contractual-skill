## 4. Objection Playbook

### Known Objection 1

OBJECTION: “Security is worried about HIPAA audit evidence.”

WHY THEY SAY THIS:  
They are not objecting to messaging itself. They are worried that approving a new patient communication vendor creates compliance, audit, and breach risk. They need proof, not promises.

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:

1. Acknowledge:
   - “That is exactly the right thing for your security team to validate.”
2. Reframe:
   - “The goal is not just HIPAA compliance in principle; it is making sure Northstar has clear evidence if anyone asks how patient communications are protected and audited.”
3. Evidence:
   - Provide HIPAA controls matrix, BAA, security architecture, audit log documentation, access control model, encryption details, incident response policy, and any third-party audit evidence available.
4. Confirm:
   - “If we map our evidence directly to your checklist, would that give security what they need to complete review?”

EXAMPLE RESPONSE:

> “Maya, I understand why security is focused on HIPAA audit evidence. For patient messaging, the question cannot be ‘Do you say you are HIPAA compliant?’ It has to be ‘Can you show the controls, logs, access policies, and agreements that prove it?’ We can provide a HIPAA evidence packet that includes our BAA, encryption and access control documentation, audit logging details, incident response process, and security questionnaire responses. The best next step is for us to get your security checklist and map our evidence directly to it. If we can get that to security by Tuesday and hold a validation call this week, would that keep the evaluation on track for June 30?”

IF THEY PUSH BACK:

- Offer direct call with your security/compliance lead.
- Ask for exact missing evidence.
- Escalate internally to provide additional documentation under NDA.
- If a formal audit report is unavailable, state that clearly and offer compensating evidence.

Do not say:
- “We are HIPAA compliant, so there should not be an issue.”
- “Other healthcare customers approved us.”
- “Security is overthinking it.”

  - Buyer requires unapproved security/legal terms.
  - Buyer wants deep discount without term, scope, or timing trade.
  - CFO cannot confirm budget or decision timeline.
