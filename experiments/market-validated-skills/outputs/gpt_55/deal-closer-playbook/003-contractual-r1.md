## Closing Strategy

### Recommended strategy: controlled escalation, not unauthorized approval

Because the deal is in negotiation with a same-day close target, the right move is to create an urgent internal approval path while avoiding unauthorized promises.

### Internal escalation package

Prepare a one-page approval request with:

1. **Deal overview**
   - BrightCart
   - $95k ARR list/pre-discount
   - Target close: today
   - Stage: negotiation

2. **Requested concessions**
   - 40% discount.
   - Unlimited support.
   - Acceptance of prospect legal terms.

3. **Approvals needed**
   - Sales manager: discount and deal strategy.
   - Finance: revenue/margin impact.
   - Legal: customer terms.
   - Support/CS: support scope and capacity.
   - Executive sponsor if exception is material.

4. **Customer commitment verification**
   - Confirm actual signer.
   - Confirm whether signature is guaranteed if approved.
   - Confirm all other blockers are removed.
   - Confirm contract document and redlines are ready.

5. **Tradeoff options for approvers**
   - Discount approval only if tied to stronger commercial terms, subject to approval.
   - Define support package rather than “unlimited support.”
   - Legal review with specific accepted/rejected redlines.
   - Multiyear term, upfront payment, case study, expansion path, or executive alignment — only if appropriate and approved.

## Negotiation Guidance

### Do not say

- “The 40% discount is approved.”
- “Unlimited support is included.”
- “Legal will accept your terms.”
- “We can guarantee signature today.”
- “This is final unless approval has actually been granted.”

### Safer alternatives

- “I’m escalating the discount request for approval.”
- “We need to define the support scope and get the right owner involved.”
- “Legal will need to review the terms before we can respond.”
- “If these are the only open items, I’ll work to get you a decision as quickly as possible.”
- “Any final package will need to reflect what is approved internally.”
