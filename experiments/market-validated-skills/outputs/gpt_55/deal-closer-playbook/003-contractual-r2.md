**Risk:** Target close date is today, but same-day close is only realistic if:

- Finance approves any discount.
- Sales leadership approves commercial terms.
- Legal approves or redlines customer terms.
- Support/customer success approves any support commitment.
- Authorized customer signer is available.

Without these, the close date should be treated as **at risk**.

## Competitive Positioning

### Facts

- No competitor was named.
- No competitive capabilities or pricing comparison was provided.

### Positioning Guidance

Because competitor information is unknown, avoid making claims such as “we are cheaper,” “we provide better support,” or “our legal terms are standard in the market.”

Instead, position around controlled value:

- Business impact of ecommerce analytics.
- Speed to actionable growth insights.
- Confidence in implementation and support scope.
- Transparent approval path for non-standard terms.
- Avoiding last-minute ambiguity that could delay rollout after signature.

### Discovery Questions

Ask Priya:

1. “Is the 40% discount tied to budget, competitor pricing, or internal ROI threshold?”
2. “Which support outcomes are required that led to the request for unlimited support?”
3. “Are there specific legal terms that are blockers?”
4. “Who is the final signer today?”
5. “If we cannot approve all three requests today, which one is the true blocker?”

## Closing Strategy

### Recommended Strategy: Parallel Approval Sprint

Because the deal is at negotiation stage with a same-day target close, the best path is not to mark concessions as approved. The best path is to run an urgent internal approval sprint.

### Internal Message To Team

> BrightCart is willing to sign today if we can resolve three non-standard requests: 40% discount, unlimited support, and acceptance of their legal terms. None are approved yet. Requesting urgent review from sales leadership, finance, legal, and support/customer success. Need approval, rejection, or approved counterposition today if possible.

### Sales Approach

1. Confirm whether Priya can influence final signature.
2. Ask BrightCart to prioritize the three asks.
3. Separate “must-have” terms from “nice-to-have” terms.
4. Escalate internally with clear business case.
5. Return only with approved positions.
