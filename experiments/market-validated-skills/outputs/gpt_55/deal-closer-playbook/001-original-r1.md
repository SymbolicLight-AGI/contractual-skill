## 4. Objection Playbook

### Known Objection 1

OBJECTION: “Security team worries about HIPAA audit evidence.”

WHY THEY SAY THIS:  
They are not just asking whether the platform is “HIPAA compliant.” They need proof they can defend during an audit or internal review. They may also be comparing you to an incumbent vendor that is already approved and therefore perceived as lower risk.

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:

1. Acknowledge:  
“You’re right to push on this. For patient messaging, the question is not just whether the platform is secure, but whether the controls and audit evidence are easy to verify.”

2. Reframe:  
“The right evaluation is: can Northstar prove who accessed PHI, what was sent, when it happened, how access is controlled, and how the vendor responds if something goes wrong?”

3. Evidence:  
Provide specific documentation:
- HIPAA control mapping
- BAA
- Audit log examples
- Access control model
- Encryption details
- Incident response policy
- Data retention/deletion policy
- Subprocessor list
- Security certifications or audit reports, if available
- Sample admin reports

4. Confirm:  
“If we provide this evidence and walk your security team through it live, would that resolve the security review concern, or are there specific audit artifacts they still need?”

EXAMPLE RESPONSE:  
“That is a fair concern, especially because this involves patient communication and PHI. We do not want your team taking our word for it. The way we usually handle this is by giving security an evidence packet that maps our controls to the questions they need to answer in a HIPAA review: audit logs, access controls, encryption, retention, breach response, BAA, and admin reporting. I’d suggest we schedule a 45-minute session with your Director of IT and security owner, walk through the evidence, and identify any remaining gaps. If they are comfortable after that, can we agree security approval moves us into legal review?”

IF THEY PUSH BACK:  
Ask for the exact evidence standard:
- “What specific HIPAA audit evidence is missing?”
- “Is there a required format or control framework?”
- “Is the concern about documentation, product capability, or vendor risk?”
- “Would a call with our security/compliance lead help close the gap?”

Escalation path:
- Bring your security/compliance lead into the next call.
- Provide redacted audit log samples.
- Offer a controlled product walkthrough focused only on compliance evidence.
- If a certification is missing, be transparent and offer compensating evidence.
