## 4. Objection Playbook

### Known Objection: Integration Effort May Be Too High

```
OBJECTION: "Integration effort may be too high"

WHY THEY SAY THIS:
Jordan is likely concerned that embedding a route optimization API into their existing systems ( TMS, dispatch software, driver apps, etc.) will require significant development time, API expertise, or system modifications that disrupt operations.

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
1. Acknowledge: "That's a completely valid concern. Any new API integration does require some development effort, and I want to make sure you have a realistic picture of what that looks like."
2. Reframe: "The question isn't whether integration takes effort — it's whether the outcome is worth it. Let me walk you through what typical integration looks like and what support we provide."
3. Evidence: [Specific proof points the rep should gather]:
   - Typical integration timeline for companies of similar size/use case [REP: Get this from engineering or case studies]
   - Documentation quality and developer resources available
   - Whether SDK or wrapper exists for common tech stacks
   - Whether professional services or implementation support is included
   - Reference customer who went live in [timeframe]
4. Confirm: "Does that help clarify what integration would look like? What systems are you most concerned about connecting?"

EXAMPLE RESPONSE:
"You know, that's one of the first things most of our customers ask. The good news is we built our API with developer experience as a priority. Most customers are up and running with basic integration in 2-3 weeks. We provide SDKs for [common languages], and our integration team will work alongside your developers during the first 30 days at no extra charge. One customer — [similar company] — told us their team had it live in their TMS within 10 days. But I want to be honest: the more complex your current setup, the more time it takes. What systems are you looking to connect this to?"

IF THEY PUSH BACK:
"I hear you. Let's not speculate — let's run a small proof of concept. If we can show you a working integration with your actual system in two weeks, you'll know exactly what effort is required before committing. Would that be helpful?"

ALTERNATIVE APPROACH IF INTEGRATION IS A GENUINE BARRIER:
Offer a managed integration service or pre-built connector if available. Position this as reducing their work, not just explaining it.
```

## 6. Closing Strategy

### Current State
**This deal is in Discovery/Early Evaluation stage despite the demo being completed.** The demo shows interest, but the deal has critical qualification gaps. The closing strategy is actually an **advancement strategy**.

### Recommended Approach

1. **Close the stakeholder gap first.** One meeting with Jordan Lee is not enough to advance. Schedule a follow-up focused entirely on identifying the full buying committee.

2. **Diagnose the integration concern.** The objection they raised is a proxy for something. Probe: "Walk me through what your current routing process looks like. Where does it break down? Who's involved in fixing it?"

3. **Establish a business case.** Ask: "What would it mean for your business if we could reduce route times by X%? Or cut fuel costs by Y%?" Quantify the cost of their current problem.

5. **Expand to technical contact.** Ask: "Who on your technical team should I connect with to discuss integration architecture?"
