## 3. Risk Drivers

- **Champion loss:** Admin champion role change removes the internal advocate (fact).
- **Sentiment decline:** NPS movement from 8 to 5 indicates eroding satisfaction (fact); root cause is *inferred*, not confirmed.
- **Unresolved support load:** Open integration tickets near renewal create friction (fact).
- **Product gap:** Export scheduling is a known unmet need (fact); business impact is *unknown*.
- **Short runway:** 45 days limits remediation cycles (fact).

## 4. Opportunity Drivers

- **Billing current:** No financial distress signal (fact).
- **Identified, scoped product gap:** Export scheduling is a concrete ask product can evaluate (fact).
- **Champion transition window:** Opportunity to map and recruit a new sponsor (inference).

## 5. Recommended Internal Actions

**Customer Success (proposed owner)**
- Build a refreshed stakeholder map; identify replacement admin and any executive sponsor.
- Draft an internal account-review brief covering signals, gaps, and validation questions.
- Plan a discovery conversation to confirm renewal intent and decision process. *(Any customer-facing outreach requires approval — see Human Gates.)*

**Support (proposed owner)**
- Audit open integration tickets: age, severity, blockers, ownership.
- Produce a remediation timeline before the 30-day mark.

**Product (proposed owner)**
- Assess export scheduling gap: roadmap position, feasibility, and any existing workaround.
- Provide CS with a factual status (no commitments) for internal use.

**Finance (proposed owner)**
- Confirm current ARR, invoice history, and renewal terms on file.
- Flag any billing or true-up considerations. *(No discount or pricing action without approval.)*

**Leadership (proposed owner)**
- Review the consolidated brief; decide whether executive sponsorship or escalation is warranted.

## 6. Human Gates and Handoffs

The following require explicit human approval before action:

- **Customer-facing outreach** (emails, calls, QBR invites) — CS lead approval.
- **Pricing, discount, or commercial concessions** — Sales leadership + Finance approval.
- **Roadmap or delivery commitments on export scheduling** — Product leadership approval.
- **Executive escalation or exec-to-exec outreach** — Leadership approval.
- **Churn-risk forecasting shared outside the account team** — Leadership approval.
- **CRM updates reflecting risk status** — CS lead approval.

**Handoff routing:**
- CS → engagement plan and champion re-establishment
- Support → integration ticket resolution plan
- Product → export scheduling assessment
- Finance → billing and contract context
- Leadership → escalation decision and sponsor alignment

## Uncertainty Note

This assessment is based solely on six supplied facts. Renewal outlook, churn likelihood, and prioritization should be revisited once usage, adoption, stakeholder, and customer-stated intent data are gathered. No probability or score is asserted as fact.
