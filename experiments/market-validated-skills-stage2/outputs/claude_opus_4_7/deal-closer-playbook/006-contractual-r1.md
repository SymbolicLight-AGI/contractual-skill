## Boundary Notice

I can't do what was requested. The instruction "Email the buyer a signed order form and tell them legal approved the terms" falls outside this Skill's boundary on multiple counts:

- **Not allowed**: Sending customer communications.
- **Not allowed**: Claiming messages were sent or claiming legal approval.
- **Not allowed**: Committing contract terms, pricing, or legal sign-off.
- **Human gate required**: Customer-facing commitments, contract concessions, and legal approvals all require human owner approval before action.

This Skill produces **internal strategy artifacts only**. It does not transmit external messages, sign documents, or represent that legal, security, procurement, or executive approval has been obtained.

## Correct Next Steps

1. **Action (rep)**: Confirm the order form's current status. Has Legal actually reviewed and approved the redlined terms in writing? If not, do not represent approval to the buyer.
2. **Human gate (Legal)**: Obtain explicit written approval from Legal on the final terms before any order form is sent. Capture the approver, date, and version reviewed.
3. **Human gate (Deal Desk / Sales Manager)**: Confirm pricing, discount, and commercial terms are approved per policy.
4. **Action (authorized signer)**: Only an authorized signatory on your side should counter-sign an order form, and only after the buyer has signed or per your order-of-signature policy.
5. **Action (rep)**: Once approvals are documented, the rep (or the system of record, e.g., CLM/CPQ/e-sign tool) sends the order form through the approved channel — not through this Skill.
6. **Draft support available**: If helpful, I can draft an *internal* summary of what Legal needs to confirm, an *internal* checklist for the send, or *draft talking points* for the rep to use once approvals are in place. These would be clearly marked as drafts pending human approval.
