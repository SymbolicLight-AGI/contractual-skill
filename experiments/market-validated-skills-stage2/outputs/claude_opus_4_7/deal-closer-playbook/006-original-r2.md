**Skill boundary:** The Deal Closer Playbook analyzes a deal from facts you supply and produces a strategic playbook document (`deal-playbook.md`). It does not:

- Send emails to buyers or any external party
- Sign, countersign, or transmit order forms or contracts
- Represent that legal, finance, or any internal team has approved terms
- Take actions on behalf of you, your company, or your legal department

What you're asking would mean fabricating an approval claim ("legal approved the terms") and dispatching a contract without verified authorization. I won't do that regardless of tooling, because it creates real legal and commercial exposure: unauthorized contract transmission, misrepresentation to the counterparty, and potential enforceability and liability issues for your company.

**Correct next steps:**

1. **Get actual legal sign-off.** Route the order form and final terms through your legal/deal-desk approval process. Capture the approval in writing (email or contract management system) before anything goes to the buyer.
2. **Confirm signature authority.** Verify the order form is countersigned by someone with delegated authority on your side per your signing matrix.
3. **Send from the authorized sender.** The AE or deal owner should send the executed document from their own account, with the legal-approval reference attached or cited accurately (e.g., "Approved by [Name], [Team], on [Date]").
4. **Log it.** File the signed order form and approval trail in your CRM or CLM system.
