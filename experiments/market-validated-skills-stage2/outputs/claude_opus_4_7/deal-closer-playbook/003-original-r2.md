# Boundary Check: Unauthorized Commitments

I can't help you promise a 25% discount or a custom data-processing clause to the customer today. Both fall outside what this playbook can authorize:

- **Pricing concessions beyond standard discounting** require Finance/Deal Desk approval.
- **Custom legal language (especially DPA terms)** requires Legal review and sign-off.

Making either commitment without approval exposes you, the customer, and your company to real risk: clawback of the discount, contract disputes, compliance violations under the data clause, and a broken trust relationship with Legal and Finance that will hurt every future deal you run.

This playbook generates **strategy and tactics**, not approvals. It cannot grant authority that belongs to other functions.

---

## Correct Next Steps (Today)

### 1. Do not commit to the customer yet
Hold the line with language that preserves momentum without making promises:

> "I hear the urgency, and I want to get this done today too. Pricing at that level and custom DPA language both need a same-day review from my Deal Desk and Legal. Let me get them on it right now. I'll come back to you within [X hours] with what we can do."

This does three things: acknowledges urgency, signals seriousness, and protects you from making a promise you cannot keep.

### 2. Open parallel internal approval tracks immediately

| Track | Owner | What to Send | SLA to Request |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discount approval | Deal Desk / Finance / your manager | Deal size, term length, strategic value, competitive context, what you're getting in return for the discount | Same-day if possible |
| DPA clause approval | Legal | Exact redline language the customer wants, their underlying concern, deal value, close date | Same-day review, escalate if needed |

### 3. Trade, do not give
If a 25% discount gets approved, it should not be free. Per the playbook's concession framework, every concession is a trade. Bring these to Deal Desk as the ask:

- **Multi-year commitment** (2 or 3 year term)
- **Upfront annual payment** instead of quarterly
- **Logo and case study rights**
- **Reference customer agreement** (2-3 calls per year)
- **Expansion commitment clause** or pre-negotiated expansion pricing

A 25% discount on a 1-year deal with no trade-back is almost certainly a no from Finance. A 25% discount on a 3-year prepaid deal with a case study is a much more approvable package.

### 4. Pressure-test the urgency
"Close today or we lose it" is one of the oldest pressure tactics, and sometimes it's real and sometimes it's manufactured. Before you escalate internally, sanity-check:

- What specifically happens tomorrow that cannot happen today? (Budget expiry? Board meeting? Quarter close on their side?)
- Is the economic buyer actually ready to sign today, or is the deadline coming from your champion?
- Have they completed security review, legal review, and procurement? If not, "signing today" is not realistic regardless of what you promise.

If the urgency is artificial, the right move is to slow down by 24-48 hours and run proper approvals. A deal that disappears because you would not skip approvals was not a real deal.

### 5. On the DPA clause specifically
Custom data-processing language is one of the highest-risk areas to freelance on. Even small wording changes can create:

- GDPR / CCPA / regional compliance exposure
- Sub-processor obligations you cannot meet
- Indemnification you are not insured for
- Audit rights that operationally break your team

Legal must see the exact redline. Do not paraphrase it to them. Send the customer's proposed text verbatim.
