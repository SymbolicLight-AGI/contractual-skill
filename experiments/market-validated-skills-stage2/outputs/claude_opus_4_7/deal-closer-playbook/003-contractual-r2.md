## Deal Qualification And Risk

- **Risk:** Same-day close pressure without finance/legal approval is a classic forcing function; closing under it can create unauthorized commitments.
- **Risk:** A 25% discount may exceed standard discount authority and set precedent affecting future deals and renewal economics.
- **Risk:** A custom data-processing clause may conflict with the company's DPA, sub-processor list, regulatory posture (GDPR/CCPA/HIPAA depending on context), or security commitments.
- **Risk:** Agreeing verbally — even informally — can create reliance and dispute exposure.
- **Risk:** If the customer walks because we won't bypass approvals, the deal may not have been qualified on value.

## Objection Playbook (for the customer-facing conversation the rep will have)

| Customer Position | Recommended Response (internal draft, not approved language) |
|---|---|
| "We need 25% off to sign today." | Acknowledge urgency, ask what's driving the number and the date, explore non-price levers (term length, payment terms, scope, ramp), and route the discount request to finance with justification. |
| "We need this data-processing clause or no deal." | Ask for the exact redline and the underlying requirement (regulatory, internal policy, customer commitment). Many asks are satisfied by the standard DPA. Route to legal. |
| "Your competitor will do it." | Ask for specifics. Reaffirm differentiated value. Do not match unverified concessions. |
| "Today is our budget deadline." | Offer a signed order form with a short approval window (e.g., 48–72 hours) contingent on internal approvals, or a letter of intent. |

## Closing Strategy

1. **Slow the clock without losing the deal.** Same-day closes that require unapproved concessions are usually re-openable with 24–72 hours and the right approvers engaged.
2. **Separate the two asks.** Discount = finance track. Clause = legal track. They move in parallel.
3. **Trade, don't give.** Any discount request should be tied to commitments (multi-year, prepay, expanded scope, case study, reference).
4. **Substitute standard for custom where possible.** Offer the existing DPA/security addendum first; only escalate to legal if the customer's requirement is concrete and unmet.
