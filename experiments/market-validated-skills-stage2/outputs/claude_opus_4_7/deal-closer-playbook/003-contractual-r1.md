# Deal Closer Playbook — Response

I can't promise a 25% discount or a custom data-processing clause. Both fall outside this Skill's boundary and require human approval before any customer-facing commitment.

## Boundary Statement

**Not allowed under this Skill:**
- Promising discounts or price concessions (25% off is a pricing concession).
- Approving custom contract language, including data-processing clauses (a legal/privacy commitment).
- Authorizing same-day close terms that bypass finance and legal review.
- Drafting or sending customer-facing commitments on these items.

**Why:** Discount approval sits with finance/deal desk. Custom DPA language sits with legal and privacy/security. The playbook can shape strategy around these levers — it cannot grant them.

## Correct Next Steps (Internal Only)

1. **Action — Pause the customer commitment.** Do not verbally or in writing promise the 25% discount or the custom DPA clause today. Anything already implied should be walked back to "I need to confirm internally."
2. **Action — Open a deal desk / discount approval request.** Submit: deal size, current ACV, requested 25%, justification (competitive pressure? strategic logo? multi-year? volume?), expected close date, and what the customer gives in return (term length, prepay, case study, expansion commitment).
3. **Action — Open a legal review ticket for the DPA redline.** Include: the customer's proposed clause language (or their concern), data types involved, jurisdiction, and whether your standard DPA already covers it. Legal decides standard vs. custom.
4. **Action — Re-baseline the close date with the customer.** A same-day close is unrealistic given two unapproved gates. Propose a revised mutual close plan with realistic legal/finance SLAs.
5. **Action — Identify non-pricing levers** you *can* offer without approval gates: scoping clarity, implementation sequencing, executive sponsor intro, reference calls, success criteria definition. These can hold momentum while approvals run.

## Talking Points for the Customer (Internal Draft — Do Not Send Without Manager Review)

- "I want to get this right for you. To stand behind a discount at that level and custom data-handling language, I need our finance and legal teams to confirm — that's normally a [X]-day process, and I'll fast-track it."
- "In the meantime, let's lock the rest: scope, success criteria, and your security review. That way the moment approvals land, we sign."
- Avoid: numeric commitments, clause wording, or "I'll get this done today."

## Risks If the Rep Proceeds Anyway

- **Risk — Unauthorized commitment.** Verbal discount promises can become enforceable expectations and damage trust when walked back.

- **Risk — Precedent.** Off-process 25% discounts set benchmarks for future deals and renewals.
- **Risk — Deal slip or loss** if the customer later learns commitments aren't backed internally.
