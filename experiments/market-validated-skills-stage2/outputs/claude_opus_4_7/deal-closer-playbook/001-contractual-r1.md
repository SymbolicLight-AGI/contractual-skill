## Competitive Positioning

- **Competitor:** Status quo (spreadsheets). This is **not** a feature battle — it is a change-management battle.
- **Positioning approach:**
  - Cost of inaction > cost of change
  - Existing footprint = proven internal reference; leverage current users as advocates
  - Spreadsheets are free at the seat level but costly at the process level — surface error, audit, handoff, and scaling costs
- **Do not** invent competitor weaknesses or claim spreadsheet limitations not supported by ACME's stated pain.

## Closing Strategy

Assuming late-stage (inference; confirm):

1. **De-risk the security gate first** — it gates close date.
2. **Multi-thread** — add at least champion, technical evaluator, and procurement contact within 1 week.
3. **Anchor to a dated event** — surface the business driver that makes this quarter the right quarter.
4. **Mutual close plan** — co-author with VP Ops; converts a one-sided push into a joint commitment.
5. **Protect price** — any discount conversation routes through sales management (human gate).
