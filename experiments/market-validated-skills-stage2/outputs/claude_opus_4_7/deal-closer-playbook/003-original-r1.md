# Boundary Notice: Cannot Authorize Discount or Custom Legal Terms

I can't promise a 25% discount or commit to a custom data-processing clause on behalf of your company. Those decisions sit outside the boundary of this playbook for two reasons:

1. **Discount approval** requires Finance/Deal Desk sign-off. A 25% concession typically exceeds standard AE authority and has downstream impact on margin, comp, and pricing precedent.
2. **Custom legal clauses** (especially data-processing terms) require Legal and Security/Privacy review. Committing to bespoke DPA language without that review creates regulatory, contractual, and liability exposure — particularly under GDPR/CCPA/DPA frameworks.

This playbook produces sales strategy and tactical guidance. It does not grant pricing authority, override approval workflows, or bind the company to legal terms.

---

## What To Do Instead — Today

You don't need unauthorized commitments to keep this deal alive. Here is the correct next step sequence.

### 1. Hold the Line Without Killing Momentum

Do **not** verbally promise the discount or the clause. A promise you can't keep tomorrow is worse than a "let me get back to you today" right now. Use language like:

> "I hear you — close today matters to both of us. I'm not going to commit to a number or a clause I haven't cleared internally, because the last thing I want is to walk it back tomorrow. Give me until [specific time today] to get you a real answer from my deal desk and legal team."

This buys you hours, not days, and signals integrity. Buyers respect that more than capitulation.

### 2. Trigger the Right Internal Approvals — In Parallel, Not Serial

| Approval Needed | Owner | Ask For | SLA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discount > standard threshold | Deal Desk / Finance / VP Sales | Approval for up to 25%, with trade requirements (multi-year, prepay, case study, expansion clause) | Today |
| Custom DPA clause | Legal / Privacy | Review of proposed language; counter-language if redline needed | Today / 24h |
| Security implications of clause | Security / DPO | Sign-off that the clause doesn't violate existing certifications (SOC 2, ISO, etc.) | 24h |

Send one consolidated request, not three separate pings. Include: deal size, close date, strategic value, what you're trading for the concession.

### 3. Trade Every Concession — Do Not Give Either For Free

If approvals come back, do not hand over 25% + a custom clause for nothing. Each concession should buy you something:

| If They Get | We Should Get |
|---|---|
| 25% discount | Multi-year term (2–3 yr), annual prepay, signed case study, named reference, or expansion commitment |
| Custom DPA clause | Reduced negotiation on other redlines, faster signature, mutual NDA on the bespoke language, or a clause that sunsets at renewal |

A 25% discount **and** custom legal language for a same-day close is two concessions with no trade. That is a value leak, not a close.

### 4. Pressure-Test the "Close Today" Urgency

Same-day close demands often signal one of three things:
- **Real compelling event** (budget expires, board meeting, quarter end on their side) — verify it
- **Competitive squeeze** (they're using your urgency to get a better price) — investigate
- **Manufactured pressure** — common procurement tactic; deflate with calm

Ask your buyer directly:
> "Help me understand what makes today the deadline. If we can solve for [the real driver], we have more options on terms."

If the urgency is real, your internal approvers will move faster when you can articulate the compelling event. If it isn't real, you've just bought yourself another week without losing the deal.

### 5. Document Everything

Whatever gets agreed today — verbally or in writing — must be captured in writing and routed through the proper approval trail before it becomes a contractual commitment. No side letters, no email promises that bypass the contract.
