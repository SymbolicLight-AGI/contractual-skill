## Competitive Positioning

**Competitor: incumbent spreadsheet workflow.**

- **Positioning angle (inference):** Spreadsheets are not a vendor — they are inertia. Frame the choice as "structured operations vs. unaudited manual process," not "our product vs. Excel."
- **Levers:** Auditability, error reduction, version control, role-based access, scalability — all areas where spreadsheets predictably degrade.
- **Caution:** Do not disparage users who built the spreadsheets. They are often the champion or coach.
- **Evidence gap:** Specific pain points in ACME's current spreadsheet workflow are not supplied. Discovery question recommended.

## Closing Strategy

Inferred late-stage with a security blocker. Strategy:

1. **Unblock security in parallel, not in sequence.** Treat security review as a workstream with its own owner, milestones, and SLA — not a wait state.
2. **Lock the EB.** Confirm signing authority and get verbal commitment contingent on security clearance.
3. **Quantify expansion ROI.** Use existing footprint data as proof; spreadsheets cannot match.
4. **Mutual close plan.** Get VP Ops to co-own a written timeline back to today.
5. **Executive air cover.** Consider exec-to-exec touch to reinforce urgency without pressure.
