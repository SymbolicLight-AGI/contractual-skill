## 4. Objection Playbook

OBJECTION: "We can't move forward until the security review is 100% complete."

WHY THEY SAY THIS:
Infosec is a hard gatekeeper. The champion cannot bypass this process and fears internal reprimand if they push too hard.

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
1. Acknowledge: Validate the importance of their security process.
2. Reframe: Shift from sequential steps to parallel processing.
3. Evidence: Note that standard legal/procurement reviews take X weeks.
4. Confirm: Ask to start parallel tracks.

EXAMPLE RESPONSE:
"I completely agree, security is the top priority and we won't finalize anything until your team is 100% comfortable. Because these reviews typically take a few weeks, and I know you want to hit your implementation timeline, does it make sense to start the vendor onboarding and legal review in parallel? That way, once security gives the green light, we aren't starting from scratch with procurement."

IF THEY PUSH BACK:
Accept the sequential process but map out the exact dates in the Mutual Close Plan so the delay is quantified.
