### 3. Risk Drivers
*   **Severe Data Blind Spot (Inference):** The lack of visibility into account health, stakeholder mapping, and usage trends is a primary risk. Without knowing if the customer is achieving ROI or experiencing support friction, the account must be treated as at-risk until validated.

### 4. Opportunity Drivers
*   **Active Usage (Fact):** The customer is currently using the product, which provides a baseline foundation for value realization and a reason to initiate an engagement conversation.

### 5. Recommended Internal Actions
*   **Data Gathering:** The account team must immediately audit internal systems (CRM, support desk, billing, product analytics) to fill in the missing signals listed in the evidence table.
*   **Account Planning:** Draft a stakeholder map to identify the economic buyer and executive sponsor.
*   **Meeting Preparation:** Prepare an agenda for a discovery call or Quarterly Business Review (QBR) to assess customer sentiment, current business goals, and potential feature gaps.

### 6. Human Gates and Handoffs
*   **Human Gates:** 
    *   *Required:* Human confirmation is required before initiating any customer-facing outreach or sending outbound messages.
    *   *Required:* Human approval is required before updating CRM records with a renewal forecast or probability score.
    *   *Required:* Human approval is required before making any pricing, discount, or contractual recommendations.
*   **Handoffs:**
    *   **Customer Success:** Hand off to CS to build an engagement plan, schedule a check-in, and gather the missing health and adoption signals.
    *   **Sales / Account Management:** Hand off to Sales to review the commercial strategy once the contract value, billing status, and renewal date are precisely identified.
