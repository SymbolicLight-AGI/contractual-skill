**Classification:** High Risk (Inferred)

### 3. Risk Drivers
*   **Stakeholder Void:** The admin champion changing roles leaves the account without a known advocate or decision-driver right before the renewal event.
*   **Declining Sentiment:** The NPS drop from 8 to 5 is a strong indicator of growing dissatisfaction, likely tied to the unresolved technical issues.
*   **Technical Friction:** Unresolved integration tickets are actively blocking value realization and creating a poor user experience.
*   **Product Limitations:** The lack of "export scheduling" is a known gap that may be a critical requirement for the new admin or broader team.
*   **Time Constraint:** 45 days is a very short window to identify a new champion, resolve technical debt, and negotiate a renewal.

### 4. Opportunity Drivers
*   **Financial Health:** Billing is current, indicating there are no active payment disputes or immediate budget freezes.
*   **Active Engagement:** The submission of NPS scores and the existence of open integration tickets show the customer is still actively using the platform and communicating with us, providing an opening for a rescue conversation.

### 5. Recommended Internal Actions
*   **Customer Success (CS):** Immediately research the account to identify the replacement admin and any broader executive sponsors. Draft a multi-threaded engagement plan to introduce the team to the new admin.
*   **Support:** Escalate the unresolved integration tickets to Tier 2/Engineering for immediate resolution. Prepare a post-mortem or status update for CS to present to the customer.
*   **Product:** Review the "export scheduling" feature request. Provide CS with a definitive roadmap update, beta access, or a viable technical workaround.
*   **Finance:** Review the account's commercial standing and prepare potential flexible renewal terms, short-term extensions, or save-desk concessions to buy time.
*   **Leadership:** Prepare an executive sponsor to reach out to the customer's leadership team to acknowledge the integration delays, apologize for the friction, and re-establish the strategic partnership.
