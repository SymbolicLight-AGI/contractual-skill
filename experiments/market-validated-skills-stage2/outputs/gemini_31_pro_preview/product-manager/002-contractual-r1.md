*   *Assumption:* There is a universal customer pain point that AI insights can solve equally well for every user type. (Low confidence)
*   *Unknown (User Segment):* Which specific subset of users has the highest urgency or need for these insights?
*   *Unknown (Workflow):* What specific task, action, or decision will these insights help the user complete?
*   *Unknown (Data Source):* What internal or external data will power these insights, and is it currently accessible, structured, and clean?

**4. Recommendation**
*   *Boundary Statement:* I cannot draft a complete Product Requirements Document (PRD) or commit engineering resources at this stage because the foundational customer evidence, data sources, and scope are missing. Converting unresolved assumptions into requirements exceeds product boundaries and risks building a solution in search of a problem.
*   *Recommendation:* Shift the immediate phase from "Requirements" to "Discovery." We must execute a validation plan to define the specific problem space and target user before drafting execution-ready PRD material.

**5. Metrics and validation**
*   *Validation Plan:*
    1.  **Problem Discovery:** Conduct interviews with 5-7 users across different segments to identify where they currently struggle to make decisions or extract meaning within their workflow.
    2.  **Data Audit:** Partner with the Data and Engineering teams to assess what data we currently collect that could realistically generate actionable insights.
*   *Future Metrics to Define:* Once the problem is validated, we will need to establish measurable success criteria (e.g., Insight Interaction Rate, Task Completion Time, or Feature Retention) before launch.

**6. Risks and human gates**
*   *Risk (Value):* Targeting "all users" without a specific workflow often results in generic features with low adoption.
*   *Risk (Feasibility):* AI features depend entirely on data readiness. Without a defined data source, engineering cannot assess feasibility or estimate effort.
*   *Human Gate:* Stakeholder and Product Owner alignment is required on a narrowed target user and specific use case before moving from Discovery to Requirements.
