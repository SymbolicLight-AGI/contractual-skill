## 6. Objection Playbook

Because no objections were provided, use this as a discovery-based objection matrix.

| Possible Objection | Discovery Response | Internal Action |
|---|---|---|
| “We’re not ready yet.” | “What needs to happen before this becomes a priority?” | Identify missing trigger, business case, or stakeholder. |
| “We don’t have budget.” | “Is this a budget issue, timing issue, or value justification issue?” | Confirm budget source and approval process. |
| “We need to evaluate alternatives.” | “What criteria will you use to compare options?” | Gather decision criteria and competitor names. |
| “Security/legal needs to review.” | “Who owns that process, and when should they be involved?” | Engage security/legal owner internally before late-stage delay. |
| “We need executive approval.” | “What does the executive team need to see to approve?” | Build value case and executive summary. |
| “The price may be too high.” | “Compared to what outcome or alternative?” | Do not offer discount. Re-anchor on quantified value. |

**Human gate:** Any pricing, discount, payment term, contract, legal, security, or implementation commitment requires approval from the appropriate internal owner.

## 8. Closing Strategy

Given the lack of required deal information, the correct strategy is **not to push for close immediately**. The correct strategy is to establish deal reality, decision process, value, and mutual timeline.

### Recommended Stage

**Inference:** This should be treated as **unqualified discovery or early qualification** until proven otherwise.

### Closing Strategy By Priority

1. **Qualify the opportunity**
   - Confirm company, use case, pain, business impact, budget, authority, timeline, and competition.

2. **Identify the buying committee**
   - Find champion, economic buyer, evaluator, procurement/legal path, and blockers.

3. **Create a mutual success hypothesis**
   - Define what success means for the customer and what business outcome they need.

4. **Establish urgency**
   - Determine whether there is a real compelling event or only seller-driven urgency.

5. **Build a mutual close plan only after qualification**
   - Do not forecast a close date until the customer confirms process, milestones, and required approvals.
