**If They Push Back:**

> “Understood. Should we park this until there is a specific initiative or deadline, or is there someone else internally who is driving this more actively?”

---

### Objection 2: “I need to talk to my boss.”

**Why They Say This:**

The current contact may not have authority or may not know how to sell this internally.

**Response Framework:**

1. Acknowledge the need for internal alignment.
2. Reframe around helping them have a stronger internal conversation.
3. Offer a business case or executive summary.
4. Ask to join the conversation.

**Example Response:**

> “Absolutely. Most teams need leadership alignment before moving forward. To make that conversation useful, would it help if we built a short summary around the business problem, expected impact, investment, and implementation plan? Also, if your boss is open to it, I am happy to join for 20 minutes and answer questions directly.”

**If They Push Back:**

> “No problem. What concerns do you expect your boss to raise, and what information would help you answer them?”

### Objection 3: “We do not have budget.”

**Why They Say This:**

The pain may not be urgent enough, the buyer may not control budget, or the timing may miss the planning cycle.

**Response Framework:**

1. Acknowledge budget reality.
2. Reframe around cost of inaction.
3. Quantify the pain.
4. Determine if there is a funded initiative this maps to.

**Example Response:**

> “I understand. If there is no budget, we should not pretend there is. The question I would ask is whether this problem is costing the business enough to justify creating or reallocating budget. Can we quantify the impact together and see whether this ties to an existing priority?”

**If They Push Back:**

> “That is fair. When is the next budget planning cycle, and who owns that process?”

### Objection 4: “Send me information.”

**Why They Say This:**

They may be interested but not committed, avoiding a meeting, or unsure who should be involved.

**Response Framework:**

1. Agree to send information.
2. Avoid a passive email-only path.

### Qualification Questions to Ask Immediately

#### Business Pain

- “What problem are you trying to solve?”
- “Why is this important now?”
- “What happens if this does not get solved?”
- “How are you handling it today?”

- “How do you measure the impact of this problem?”
- “Is this tied to revenue, cost reduction, risk reduction, productivity, customer experience, or compliance?”
- “Can we estimate the cost of doing nothing?”

#### Buyer / Authority

- “Who owns this initiative?”
- “Who controls budget?”
- “Who signs off on this type of purchase?”
- “Who else needs to be involved before a decision is made?”

#### Decision Criteria

- “How will you evaluate whether a solution is the right fit?”
- “What are the must-haves versus nice-to-haves?”
- “Are you comparing vendors, building internally, or deciding whether to do anything at all?”

#### Decision Process

- “What steps would need to happen between now and approval?”
- “Is there a security, legal, procurement, or vendor review process?”
- “How long does that usually take?”
- “Have you bought something like this before?”

#### Timeline

- “Is there a date you are trying to hit?”
- “What is driving that date?”
- “If you wanted to be live by then, when would you need to decide?”
- “Is this tied to a budget cycle, board meeting, launch, renewal, compliance deadline, or strategic initiative?”

#### Champion

- “Who internally cares most about solving this?”
- “Who would be willing to advocate for this?”
- “If you wanted to move forward, who would help make the case?”
