Do **not** promise the 25% discount or the custom data-processing clause today without finance and legal approval.

That exceeds the deal-closing boundary: you can negotiate directionally, but you cannot make unauthorized pricing or legal commitments on behalf of the company.

## Recommended Response to the Prospect

Use this:

> “I understand that the 25% discount and the custom data-processing clause are important to getting this finalized. I want to be careful not to overpromise something that requires approval from our finance and legal teams.  
>   
> What I can do today is take both requests back internally as priority items and get you a confirmed answer by [specific time/date].  
>   
> To make sure I advocate for the right package, can we confirm that if we can reach agreement on commercial terms and legal language, you are prepared to move forward with signature today / by [date]?”

## What the Rep Should Do Next

1. **Do not send revised pricing or contract language yet.**
   - No email, proposal, order form, Slack screenshot, or verbal commitment implying approval.

2. **Get conditional commitment from the buyer.**
   Ask:
   > “If we can get approval on the discount and align on the data-processing language, is there anything else that would prevent signature today?”

3. **Escalate internally immediately.**
   Send finance/legal this summary:
   - Deal size
   - Requested 25% discount
   - Business reason for discount
   - Contract term length
   - Close date urgency
   - Requested custom data-processing clause
   - Whether buyer has given conditional signature commitment
   - Any competitive pressure or procurement deadline

4. **Trade, do not give.**
   If finance considers the discount, ask for something in return:
   - Multi-year contract
   - Larger upfront payment
   - Annual prepay
   - Faster signature date
   - Reference/case study rights
   - Reduced implementation concessions
   - Expansion commitment

5. **Route the custom data-processing clause through legal only.**
   The rep should not edit, accept, or summarize legal obligations independently.

## Approved Positioning to Use While Waiting

> “I’m working to get you a clean answer rather than giving you a soft yes that creates issues later. My goal is to get this approved correctly so there are no surprises during procurement or implementation.”

## Red Lines

Do **not** say:

- “Yes, we can do 25%.”
- “I’m sure legal will be fine with that.”
- “Send it over and we’ll accept it.”
- “We can make the data-processing language work.”
- “If you sign today, I’ll get this approved after.”

## Best Close Move

Convert the request into a conditional close:

> “Assuming we can get approval on the commercial and legal items, are you ready to sign today? If yes, I’ll escalate this now as a final approval request rather than an open-ended negotiation.”
