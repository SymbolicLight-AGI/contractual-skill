## Objection Playbook

### 1. Security Questionnaire Is Pending

**Likely customer concern:**  
“We need InfoSec approval before we can proceed.”

**Internal response strategy:**  
- Assign a clear internal security owner immediately.
- Confirm questionnaire format, due date, and required supporting documents.
- Reuse approved security artifacts where available.
- Track unanswered items and escalation paths.

**Suggested customer-facing positioning, subject to approval:**  
“We’re coordinating with our security/compliance team to complete the questionnaire accurately. To avoid delays, can you confirm the required due date, review process, and any mandatory artifacts your team needs?”

**Do not promise:**  
- Security approval.
- Specific questionnaire completion date unless approved by security owner.
- Compliance certifications unless verified.

---

### 2. Procurement Vendor Onboarding

**Likely customer concern:**  
“We can’t issue a PO or contract until you’re onboarded as a vendor.”

**Internal response strategy:**  
- Ask for onboarding checklist or portal access.
- Confirm whether vendor setup requires tax forms, insurance certificates, banking info, security documentation, legal review, or supplier diversity forms.
- Identify internal owner for each required item.
- Track procurement SLA and dependencies.

**Suggested customer-facing positioning, subject to approval:**  
“We can support the vendor onboarding process. Please send the onboarding checklist or portal instructions, and we’ll coordinate the required internal owners.”

**Do not promise:**  
- Completion of onboarding by a specific date without internal confirmation.
- Acceptance of procurement terms.
- Changes to payment terms, insurance language, or legal provisions.

---

### 3. Implementation Timeline Request

**Likely customer concern:**  
“If we buy, how quickly can we launch?”

**Internal response strategy:**  
- Treat this as a positive buying signal but also a risk area.
- Do not send an implementation timeline until CS/implementation confirms scope assumptions.
- Prepare a draft timeline with assumptions and dependencies, clearly labeled non-binding.
- Ask discovery questions before committing timeline.

**Questions to clarify before responding externally:**
- What is the desired go-live date?
- How many users/teams are included in phase 1?
- Are integrations, SSO, data migration, custom configuration, or admin training required?
- Who is the customer-side implementation owner?
- Are there blackout dates, change control windows, or internal launch dependencies?
- Is implementation expected before or after contract signature?

**Do not promise:**  
- Go-live date.
- Implementation duration.
- Resource availability.
- Integration feasibility.
- Business outcomes.
