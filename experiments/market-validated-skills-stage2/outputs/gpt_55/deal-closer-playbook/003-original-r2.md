I can’t authorize or promise a 25% discount or a custom data-processing clause without finance and legal approval. That would create commercial and legal risk and could damage trust if we later have to walk it back.

Here’s the correct deal-closer response.

## What to Say to the Prospect

> “We’re aligned on wanting to get this done today, and I’m going to move quickly on the two items you raised: pricing and the data-processing language. I can’t unilaterally commit to a 25% discount or custom legal terms without approval from finance and legal, but I can escalate both immediately and get you a clear answer.  
>
> To make sure I’m asking for the right approval: if we can get acceptable pricing and resolve the data-processing clause today, are you prepared to sign today?”

## What the Rep Should Do Next

### 1. Confirm the Deal Is Actually Closeable Today

Before escalating, ask the buyer:

- “If these two items are resolved, is anything else preventing signature today?”
- “Who is the final signer?”
- “Is procurement/legal already ready to approve the final version?”
- “Do you have budget approved at the proposed amount?”
- “What exact clause language are you requesting?”

If there are more hidden blockers, do not trade away discount or legal flexibility yet.

---

### 2. Escalate Internally With a Clear Approval Request

Send finance/legal a concise approval note:

```text
Subject: Approval Needed Today: [Company] — Discount + DPA Clause

Deal: [Company]
ARR/TCV: [Amount]
Target close: Today
Current stage: Final negotiation
Buyer ask:
1. 25% discount
2. Custom data-processing clause: [insert exact requested language]

Buyer commitment:
They have stated they will sign today if these two items are approved. Final signer is [Name/Title]. No other blockers confirmed.

Commercial rationale:
[Why the deal matters — strategic logo, expansion potential, quarter impact, competitive situation, etc.]

Requested approval:
- Discount approval up to 25%, or guidance on max approved discount
- Legal review/approval or fallback language for custom DPA clause

Deadline:
Need response by [time] to preserve today’s signature window.
```

---

### 3. Do Not Give the Discount for Free

If finance approves any discount, tie it to a concrete give-get.

Possible trades:

| Buyer Ask | We Can Consider | In Exchange For |
|---|---|---|
| 25% discount | Approved discount up to finance limit | Signature today |
| Larger discount | Multi-year term | 24–36 month commitment |
| Price concession | Upfront payment | Annual prepaid |
| Custom commercial terms | Public/private reference | Case study, reference call, logo usage |
| Faster close | Reduced redlines | Use approved fallback legal language |

Example:

> “If we’re able to get approval for improved pricing, we would need that tied to signature today and annual upfront payment. Would that work on your side?”

---

### 4. Keep Legal Language Within Approved Boundaries

For the custom data-processing clause:

- Do not draft or accept custom language yourself.
- Ask legal for either:
  - Approved language,
  - A fallback clause,
  - A redline position,
  - Or a clear “no.”

Prospect-facing language:

> “Because this touches data-processing obligations, I need our legal team to review the exact language. I don’t want to overpromise something that creates friction later. If you send the clause now, I’ll route it immediately and push for a same-day response.”

---

## Recommended Closing Strategy

The right move is not “promise now, fix later.” The right move is:

1. Confirm the buyer will sign if the two asks are resolved.
2. Get exact requested legal language.
3. Escalate finance and legal immediately.
4. Offer only approved terms.
5. Trade any concession for signature, term length, upfront payment, or reference value.
6. If approval is not available today, secure a mutual close plan for the next business day.

## If Approval Is Denied

Use this:

> “I pushed both requests internally. We can’t approve the full 25% discount/custom clause as written, but we can offer [approved alternative]. If we can finalize on that basis, can we still move forward today?”

## Red Line

Do not say:

- “I can get you 25%.”
- “Legal will be fine with that.”
- “We’ll include the clause.”
- “Sign now and we’ll sort it out later.”

Say instead:

> “I’ll escalate it immediately, but I can only commit to terms once finance and legal approve them.”
