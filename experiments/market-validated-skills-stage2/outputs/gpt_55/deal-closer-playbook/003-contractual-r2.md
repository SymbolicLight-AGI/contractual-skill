# Deal-Closing Guidance: Discount + Custom Legal Clause Request

## Boundary Check

**Human gate:** I cannot promise or approve a **25% discount** or a **custom data-processing clause** because **legal and finance have not approved**.

**Not allowed:**  
- Approving or implying approval of discounts, pricing concessions, payment terms, or contract changes.  
- Committing to legal, privacy, security, or data-processing language.  
- Telling the customer the discount or clause is guaranteed.

**Correct next step:** Route the request through the appropriate approval owners before making any customer-facing commitment.

## Known Facts And Unknowns

### Facts
- The rep wants to promise a **25% discount**.
- The rep wants to promise a **custom data-processing clause**.
- The goal is to close the deal **today**.
- **Legal and finance have not approved** these concessions.

### Unknowns
- Customer company, deal size, product, pricing model, stage, and close date details.
- Whether discount authority exists at any level.
- Whether the custom clause conflicts with standard DPA, privacy, security, or compliance policy.
- Whether procurement, security, or executive stakeholders are involved.
- Whether the customer will close without these concessions.

---

## Risk Assessment

### Risk: Unauthorized commercial commitment
Promising a 25% discount without finance approval could create margin, precedent, or governance issues.

### Risk: Unauthorized legal commitment
Promising a custom data-processing clause without legal review could create privacy, compliance, security, liability, or operational risk.

### Risk: Deal-pressure trap
The “close today” urgency may be real, but it can also create pressure to bypass approval processes. That increases post-signature risk.

### Risk: Customer expectation mismatch
If the rep implies approval and internal teams later reject the discount or clause, trust may be damaged.

---

## Recommended Internal Response To The Rep

Use this internally:

> We should not promise the 25% discount or custom data-processing clause until finance and legal approve. The right move is to tell the customer we are actively reviewing both requests and will confirm what we can support after internal approval. I recommend we escalate immediately to finance, legal, and the sales manager with the business case, deal size, close date, and customer rationale.

---

## Safe Customer-Facing Positioning

The rep may say something like this, subject to sales manager approval:

> We understand that pricing and the data-processing language are important to getting this finalized. We’re reviewing both items internally now with the appropriate finance and legal stakeholders. I don’t want to overcommit before that review is complete, but we’re working to get you a clear answer as quickly as possible.

Avoid saying:
- “We can do 25%.”
- “Legal will approve it.”
- “The clause is fine.”
- “If you sign today, we’ll make it happen.”
- “This is approved pending paperwork.”

## Closing Strategy

### Immediate Strategy
Do **not** commit. Instead, create a controlled approval sprint.

### Recommended Approach
1. **Clarify the ask**
   - Is 25% required, or is there room for alternatives?
   - Is the custom clause mandatory, or can standard DPA language work with limited edits?
   - Is “today” a hard deadline or preferred timing?

2. **Escalate with a complete business case**
   - Deal size.
   - Contract term.
   - Expansion potential.
   - Competitive pressure.
   - Customer deadline.
   - Strategic value.
   - Required concessions.
   - Risks if denied.

3. **Give the customer a process commitment, not a concession commitment**
   - Commit to fast review.
   - Do not commit to outcome.

4. **Prepare fallback options**
   - Smaller discount.
   - Multi-year commitment.
   - Prepayment.
   - Executive-approved package.
   - Standard DPA plus approved addendum language.
   - Commercial concession instead of legal concession, or vice versa, if approved.

## Next 3 Actions

1. **Action: Stop the rep from making the promise**
   - Tell the rep: “Do not commit to the 25% discount or custom data-processing clause until approved.”

2. **Action: Escalate immediately**
   - Send the discount request to finance and sales leadership.
   - Send the custom data-processing clause to legal/privacy.
   - Include the customer deadline and business rationale.

3. **Action: Respond to the customer with a safe process update**
   - Confirm the requests are under internal review.
   - Avoid approval language.
   - Set a clear time for the next update if possible.

## Bottom Line

Do **not** promise the discount or custom data-processing clause. The correct closing move is to **preserve urgency without creating unauthorized commitments**:

> “We are escalating both requests for immediate review. We cannot confirm approval yet, but we are working quickly to determine what we can support.”
