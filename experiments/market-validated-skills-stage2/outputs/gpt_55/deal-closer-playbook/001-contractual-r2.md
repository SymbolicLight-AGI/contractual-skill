## 8. Closing Strategy

### Strategy Theme

**Turn the deal from a software expansion into an operational risk and efficiency decision, while removing IT security as the critical-path blocker.**

### Stage-Based Guidance

Current stage is **[UNKNOWN]**, so use this as a late-discovery / validation / closing motion until confirmed.

### Tactical Plan

1. **Confirm business case with VP Operations**
   - Tie the expansion to specific operational outcomes.
   - Quantify spreadsheet pain.
   - Ask what happens if ACME delays one or two quarters.

2. **Isolate and unblock IT security**
   - Identify the exact owner and open items.
   - Create a security review action plan.
   - Provide only approved security materials.
   - Do not promise security approval or timelines without security owner confirmation.

3. **Map approval path**
   - Confirm final approver, procurement steps, legal steps, and order form process.
   - Determine whether this is under an existing agreement or requires new terms.

4. **Create urgency around quarter-close**
   - Avoid artificial pressure.
   - Use operational cost of delay and ACME’s desired timeline.
   - Ask VP Ops what internal milestone requires this to be completed this quarter.

5. **Enable the VP Operations buyer**
   - Provide an internal business case summary.
   - Prepare concise responses for IT, finance, and leadership.
   - Confirm whether they will sponsor the expansion internally.

## 11. Negotiation Guidance

### Recommended Approach

- Anchor negotiation on operational value, not price.
- Avoid discounting to overcome security delay; security is a process blocker, not necessarily a price objection.
- Do not offer pricing concessions without sales manager approval.
- If budget pressure emerges, explore scope, timing, or phased rollout only after internal approval.
- Use the existing customer relationship, if applicable, to streamline procurement, but do not assume existing terms apply without confirmation.

### Human Gates

- **Discounts or payment terms:** Require sales manager / finance approval.
- **Contract terms:** Require legal approval.
- **Security commitments:** Require security/privacy approval.
- **Implementation timelines:** Require delivery/customer success approval.
- **Executive escalation:** Require sales leadership alignment.
