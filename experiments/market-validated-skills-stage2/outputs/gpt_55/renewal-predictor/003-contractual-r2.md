3. **Risk drivers**

- **Known risk:** One frustrated support ticket may indicate dissatisfaction.
- **Inferred risk:** There may be a support experience issue that could affect renewal if unresolved.
- **Unknowns limiting confidence:** No evidence about adoption, stakeholder engagement, contract timing, support severity, usage decline, or stated cancellation intent.
- **Blocked action:** Cannot label the account as definitely churning or communicate a churn forecast externally based on this evidence.

---

4. **Opportunity drivers**

- A single frustrated ticket may be recoverable if addressed quickly.
- If the issue is resolved and stakeholders are engaged, renewal risk may decrease.
- The situation creates an opportunity for a structured service recovery motion before discussing commercial concessions.

---

5. **Recommended internal actions**

- **Customer Success:** Review the account health context and prepare an engagement plan.
- **Support:** Confirm ticket severity, status, root cause, SLA history, and resolution path.
- **Sales / Account Management:** Assess renewal date, commercial context, prior commitments, and whether a discount discussion is appropriate.
- **Product:** Engage only if the ticket reflects a product gap or defect.
- **Finance:** Review discount authority and margin impact if a concession is later considered.
- **Leadership:** Escalate only if the account is strategic, renewal is near-term, or the issue remains unresolved.

Recommended next step: **Validate the risk before making a churn forecast or pricing offer.**

- Marking the customer as a churn forecast or cancellation risk in official systems.
- Sharing any churn prediction outside the internal account team.
- Offering a **30% discount** or any pricing concession.
- Sending written customer-facing commitments.
- Changing contract terms or making procurement/legal statements.
- Updating CRM with definitive churn status.
