## 1. Renewal outlook

**Renewal health classification: At-risk / moderate-to-high renewal risk.**  
This is an **inferred outlook**, not a factual churn prediction.

The account has several meaningful renewal risk signals: renewal is approaching in **75 days**, usage is declining, support volume is increasing, and the executive sponsor has left. However, there are also stabilizing signals: the admin champion remains engaged and two key workflows are adopted, indicating some ongoing value and a potential path to recovery.

**Primary renewal concern:** stakeholder risk plus declining engagement close to renewal.  
**Primary recovery path:** re-establish executive sponsorship, validate workflow value, resolve support friction, and build a renewal plan with the engaged admin champion.

## 3. Risk drivers

1. **Declining usage**
   - Weekly active users are down **18%**.
   - This suggests possible adoption fatigue, reduced business value, workflow friction, or user churn within the account.
   - This is a risk signal, but the cause is unknown.

2. **Increased support activity**
   - Support tickets are up.
   - This may indicate product issues, enablement gaps, configuration problems, or scaling friction.
   - Without severity, resolution status, or themes, this should be treated as an unresolved risk requiring review.

3. **Executive sponsor departure**
   - The executive sponsor has left.
   - This creates risk around budget ownership, strategic alignment, and renewal advocacy.
   - A new executive sponsor or economic buyer should be identified quickly.

4. **Renewal is close**
   - 75 days leaves limited time for executive re-alignment, issue remediation, and value confirmation.
   - The account needs a structured internal renewal plan now.

---

## 4. Opportunity drivers

1. **Admin champion remains engaged**
   - This is a strong positive signal.
   - The admin champion can help validate use cases, identify pain points, map stakeholders, and support a renewal path.

2. **Two key workflows are adopted**
   - The product is not unused or purely experimental.
   - Existing workflow adoption can be used to anchor a value review and identify expansion or retention themes.

3. **Usage decline may be recoverable**
   - An 18% decline is material, but not necessarily irreversible.
   - If the decline is tied to specific teams, workflows, or unresolved issues, targeted intervention may improve the outlook before renewal.

## 5. Recommended internal actions

### Customer Success
- Build a **75-day renewal recovery plan** with weekly checkpoints.
- Meet with the engaged admin champion to validate:
  - reasons for usage decline,
  - health of the two adopted workflows,
  - unresolved pain points,
  - stakeholder changes after the executive sponsor departure.
- Prepare a value review focused on the adopted workflows and measurable business outcomes, using only validated account data.

### Sales / Account Management
- Start stakeholder mapping immediately.
- Identify the new executive sponsor, economic buyer, procurement contact, and decision process.
- Prepare commercial strategy internally, but do **not** offer pricing, discounts, or renewal terms without approval.

### Support
- Review recent ticket trends:
  - ticket volume change,
  - severity,
  - open vs. closed status,
  - recurring themes,
  - SLA exposure,
  - customer-impacting blockers.
- Recommend a remediation plan if tickets reveal unresolved friction.

### Product
- If support tickets or admin feedback indicate feature gaps, assess whether these are:
  - known limitations,
  - configuration/training issues,
  - roadmap dependencies,
  - true renewal blockers.
- Do not commit roadmap timing externally without product and leadership approval.

### Finance
- Confirm billing status, payment history, and any outstanding invoices.
- Assess whether there is billing-related renewal exposure.

### Leadership
- Consider internal executive alignment if the account is strategically important due to $120k ARR and sponsor loss.
- Any executive outreach should be approved before being sent.

## 6. Human gates and handoffs

Human approval is required before any of the following:

- Sending customer-facing renewal or escalation messages.
- Offering discounts, concessions, or pricing guidance.
- Making contractual, legal, procurement, or renewal-term commitments.
- Communicating churn forecasts outside the internal account team.
- Updating CRM records with inferred risk labels or next steps.
- Promising product roadmap items, support outcomes, or executive involvement.

| Team | Handoff purpose |

| Leadership | Approve executive escalation if needed. |
