## Active Churn Signals

- **Executive sponsor departure:** The executive sponsor has left, creating a decision-maker gap ahead of renewal.
- **Usage decline near renewal:** Weekly active users are down 18% with renewal only 75 days away.
- **Support volume increase:** Ticket volume is rising, which may indicate product friction or operational dissatisfaction.
- **Renewal proximity risk:** The account renews within 90 days, so unresolved risk signals are more urgent.

## Positive / Retention Signals

- **Admin champion still engaged:** This gives the CS team an internal advocate to help rebuild stakeholder alignment.
- **Two key workflows adopted:** The product has some embedded value, which can be used as the foundation for a renewal value narrative.

## Recommended Save Play

**Priority:** High  
**Primary Action:** Stakeholder Re-engagement Campaign + Executive Sponsor Alignment  
**Owner:** CSM with CS Leader support  
**Deadline:** Within 10 business days; executive outreach should begin within 5 business days  
**Expected Outcome:** Identify and brief the new executive sponsor or decision-maker, confirm renewal priorities, and secure agreement on a 60-day renewal success plan.

### Specific Next Steps

1. **Ask the admin champion for an introduction to the new executive sponsor or budget owner.**
   - Goal: Re-establish executive alignment before procurement or renewal discussions begin.

2. **Prepare a value summary around the two adopted workflows.**
   - Include usage history, business outcomes, workflow dependency, and any efficiency gains.

3. **Audit recent support tickets.**
   - Identify whether ticket growth is due to bugs, usability friction, training gaps, or increased usage.
   - Assign a support owner if any tickets are high severity or aging.

4. **Schedule a renewal risk review meeting within 10 business days.**
   - Attendees: Admin champion, CSM, CS leader, support lead if needed, and new executive sponsor if identified.

5. **Create a 60-day mutual success plan.**
   - Focus areas: stabilize usage, resolve support issues, expand stakeholder coverage, and document renewal value.

## Risk if Delayed

If executive alignment is not restored quickly, the renewal may become dependent on a single admin champion without budget authority. Combined with declining WAU and increasing support tickets, this could lead to a late-stage renewal objection, downgrade request, or competitive review.
