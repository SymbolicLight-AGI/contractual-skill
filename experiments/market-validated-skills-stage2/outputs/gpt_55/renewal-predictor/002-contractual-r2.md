3. Risk drivers

- Insufficient evidence to classify renewal health.
- No usage trend or adoption depth provided.
- No stakeholder or executive sponsor visibility.
- No support, billing, sentiment, or contract-value data.
- Renewal is this year, so discovery should happen soon to avoid late-stage surprise risk.

4. Opportunity drivers

- The customer does use the product, which is a positive starting signal.
- If usage is active, broad, and tied to business outcomes, renewal outlook could improve.
- If stakeholders confirm value realization and no major blockers exist, the account may be stable.

5. Recommended internal actions

- Customer Success: Validate adoption, business outcomes, renewal timeline, stakeholder map, and current sentiment.
- Sales / Account Management: Confirm contract value, renewal process, procurement timeline, and commercial owner.
- Support: Check for open or recurring issues that could affect renewal.
- Finance: Confirm billing status and any payment or invoicing concerns.
- Product: Identify whether any requested features or product gaps are material to renewal.
- Leadership: Only consider escalation if discovery reveals executive sponsor loss, major dissatisfaction, or strategic revenue exposure.

Suggested internal discovery questions:
- How frequently is the customer using the product?
- Is usage increasing, stable, or declining?
- Which teams or users are active?
- What business outcomes has the customer achieved?
- Who owns the renewal decision?
- Is there an active executive sponsor or champion?
- Are there unresolved support or product issues?
- Are there billing, procurement, or legal blockers?
- Has the customer expressed renewal intent, concern, or dissatisfaction?

6. Human gates and handoffs

Human approval is required before:
- Sending any customer-facing renewal message.
- Sharing a churn forecast outside the internal account team.
- Offering discounts or pricing guidance.
- Making contractual, legal, procurement, or executive commitments.
- Updating CRM renewal stage or forecast based only on this sparse data.

Recommended handoff:
- Customer Success should lead discovery and engagement planning.
- Sales / Account Management should own commercial strategy once contract value and renewal process are known.
- Support, Finance, Product, Legal, and Leadership should be engaged only if relevant risks are identified.
