### 1) Current Context Review

No project files, documentation, prior decisions, data model, analytics stack, or user research were provided.

The only product note is:

> “Build an analytics dashboard for managers.”

### What Already Exists

Known:

- A dashboard is being considered.
- The intended audience is described only as “managers.”
- The dashboard is expected to be analytics-oriented.

Unknown:

- Which managers are meant: people managers, sales managers, operations managers, product managers, finance managers, etc.
- What decisions the dashboard should support.
- Which metrics matter.
- What data sources exist.
- Whether this is internal-only or customer-facing.
- Required freshness, accuracy, security, permissions, and reliability.
- What success looks like.

### Implicit but Unconfirmed Constraints

These are not decisions yet — only possible constraints that need validation:

- Managers likely need the dashboard to make recurring operational or strategic decisions.
- The dashboard may need role-based access if different managers see different teams or business units.
- Data quality and trust may be as important as visualization.
- The scope could easily become too broad unless tied to a specific decision workflow.
- “Analytics dashboard” may mean anything from a simple KPI page to a full exploratory BI system.

## Initial Gap Assessment

Before any design is possible, we need to clarify:

### Purpose

- What problem does this dashboard solve?
- What decisions should it improve?
- What happens today without it?

### Target Users

- Which type of managers?
- How often will they use it?
- Are they executives, team leads, operations managers, customer success managers, or another group?

### Metrics

- What metrics should be shown?
- Are these strategic KPIs, operational alerts, team performance metrics, financial metrics, or product usage metrics?

### Data Sources

- Where does the data come from?
- Is it already clean and available?
- Who owns the data?

### Success Criteria

- How will we know the dashboard worked?
- Faster decision-making?
- Reduced manual reporting?
- Increased metric visibility?
- Better business outcomes?

### Non-Functional Requirements Still Missing

We will need to clarify or define assumptions for:

- Performance expectations
- Scale: number of users, dashboards, metrics, and data volume
- Security and privacy requirements
- Reliability and availability expectations
- Maintenance and ownership model
