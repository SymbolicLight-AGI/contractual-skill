## 4. Objection Playbook

### Known Objection: IT Security Review
**OBJECTION:** “Before we can expand, our IT security team needs to review and approve the new integration.”

**WHY THEY SAY THIS:** IT has a mandate to protect corporate data and ensure third‑party tools meet their policies. Probably concerns about data handling, access controls, or network impact.

**RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:**
1. **Acknowledge:** “I completely respect that. Security reviews are critical, especially in manufacturing where uptime and data integrity are non‑negotiable.”
2. **Reframe:** “We actually view security as a partnership. Our existing relationship with ACME already passed your initial review, and this expansion uses the same underlying architecture.”
3. **Evidence:** Provide up‑to‑date certifications (SOC2 Type II, ISO 27001), a summary of data flow, and reference from a similar manufacturing customer who cleared a similar review in <X days>.
4. **Confirm:** “Would it help if I sent our security documentation and a brief technical call with your lead to walk through the specifics? What does their typical timeline look like?”

**IF THEY PUSH BACK:** The review requires a penetration test or additional questionnaire. Offer to fill out the exact questionnaire immediately and, if possible, schedule a live demo of the security controls. If the process is long, propose a provisional approval with a technical POC.

### Anticipated Objections

#### Price / Budget
**OBJECTION:** “$80K is a lot for replacing spreadsheets. Our existing method is free.”

**RESPONSE:** “Spreadsheets feel free, but they carry hidden costs in errors, rework, and management time. We’ve found similar operations teams recoup the investment in 6‑9 months just through error reduction. I can share a simple model showing that for ACME’s volume, the break‑even is [X] months.”

#### Timing
**OBJECTION:** “Can we start this next quarter? We’re swamped.”

**RESPONSE:** “I understand the workload. The reason I’m raising it now is that if we start the security review this week, you could have the workflow live before the next peak period. Waiting pushes the benefit out by 90 days. What’s the one thing that would make you comfortable to move the review ahead now?”

#### Security Gaps
**OBJECTION:** “Our security team asked for an on‑premises deployment. Do you offer that?”

**RESPONSE:** “We don’t deploy on‑prem, but we have a dedicated private cloud environment that can meet strict data residency and encryption requirements. I’ll connect our security architect with your team to design a solution that matches your policy.”

#### Adoption / Change Management
**OBJECTION:** “Our floor supervisors won’t adopt a new tool.”

**RESPONSE:** “That’s a fair concern. We’ve built the interface to be simpler than their current spreadsheet. Would it help to run a 15‑minute proof walkthrough with two supervisors next week? I bet they’ll be our best internal evangelists once they see the time it saves.”

## 5. Competitive Positioning

This deal does not face a conventional vendor competitor; it competes against the **status quo: incumbent spreadsheet workflow**. This is a classic “do nothing” / internal inertia scenario.

### Competitor: Spreadsheet Workflow (Manual Processes)
**THEIR LIKELY PITCH (implied by the internal defender):** “It works, it’s free, people know how to use it, no new training, no IT review hassle.”

**WHERE THEY WIN:**
- Zero incremental cost (in the short term)
- Familiarity – zero learning curve
- No procurement or security gate required

**WHERE WE WIN:**
- Elimination of double‑data‑entry and version‑control chaos
- Real‑time visibility into production metrics (impossible in static spreadsheets)
- Audit trail and compliance (important for quality/regulatory)
- Scalability – as ACME grows, spreadsheets break; our platform scales
- Integration with existing systems (ERP, MES), reducing manual exports

**LANDMINE QUESTIONS (to ask the VP Ops and end users):**
1. “Last week, how much time did the team spend merging spreadsheet versions or fixing formula errors?”
2. “If you had a real‑time dashboard instead of a static report, what decisions would you make faster?”
3. “If an auditor asked for a full change log of those critical production numbers, how long would it take to produce?”
4. “When a key person on the spreadsheet leaves or goes on leave, what happens to the process knowledge?”

**IF THE PROSPECT FAVORS THE STATUS QUO:**
- Do not push purely on fear of error; instead, quantify the cost of the last known mistake (e.g., “the $30K rework incident last quarter”).
- Offer a limited pilot: replace one spreadsheet workflow for a specific production line, measure impact, then expand. That sidesteps the all‑or‑nothing argument.
- Use the IT security blocker as a reason to expedite: “Let’s get the security review done now so when the team is ready, there’s zero lag.”

**TRAP TO AVOID:**
- Trashing spreadsheets too hard—people who built them feel attacked. Instead, frame our solution as an “upgrade” that retains the flexibility people love but adds control and scale.

   *Expected metric impact:* Zero findings on traceability, reduced compliance prep cost by 50%.

## 9. Negotiation Strategy (Expansion)

### Our Position
- **Ideal outcome:** $80K ARR on a 1‑year expansion, with a 30‑day implementation, no discount.
- **Acceptable outcome:** $80K ARR, but willing to offer free implementation or first‑month ramp credit (up to $5K) in exchange for a multi‑year commitment on the entire account.
- **Walk‑away point:** If the deal shrinks below $60K ARR with extended payment terms (Net 90) and heavy customization, the economics no longer justify the operational cost.

### Concession Strategy

| If They Ask For | We Can Offer | In Exchange For |
|---|---|---|
| Discount on $80K | Up to 8% discount (=$73.6K ARR) | Case study participation, logo rights, or a 2‑year renewal on the whole account. |
| Net 60 payment terms | Net 45 maximum | Larger expansion (include another module) or upfront partial payment. |
| Waive implementation fee | Reduced‑scope implementation (basic configuration) | 3‑year contract on the new module. |
| Additional user training | 2 extra virtual sessions | Reference call commitment within 90 days post‑go‑live. |
| Pilot before full expansion | 3‑month paid pilot at $20K | Upon success, automatic conversion to the full $80K at the end of the pilot; no additional negotiation. |

### Red Lines (Do Not Concede)
- Wall‑to‑wall discount exceeding 15%—devalues both the expansion and the existing account.
- Full refund clause if productivity targets aren’t met (tying payment to subjective outcomes undermines the relationship).
- On‑prem deployment — not part of our offering; offer private cloud instead.
- Accepting security “conditional” that requires us to redesign core architecture; we’ll walk rather than build a bespoke version.
