I cannot promise a 25% discount or a custom data‑processing clause without explicit approval from Finance and Legal. Doing so would be unauthorized, could undermine deal credibility, and creates risk for both your company and the prospect when we can’t deliver.

Here’s what you should do right now to keep momentum while staying within your authority:

**1. Buy time without killing urgency**
Say to the prospect:  
*“I want to be completely fair with you. I need to confirm our absolute best price on this structure before I make a commitment. I can get you an answer by [specific time, e.g., 3pm tomorrow] — and if we can make the numbers work, we’ll still hit your close deadline. Does that work?”*

That maintains the timeline and positions you as honest, not obstructive.

**2. Unpack the real reason they need a 25% discount right now**
Often a big discount request is a mask for another concern: budget approval, competitor pricing, or perceived value gap. Before you go back to Finance, run this discovery:
- “Help me understand — if the price were aligned, is everything else in place to sign?”
- “What benchmark are you measuring 25% against?”  
- “Is the discount the only blocker, or are there other terms you’d need reviewed?”

Use the answers to either separate the discount from the legal clause or find where you can offer value instead of margin.

**3. For the custom data‑processing clause**
That is 100% a Legal issue. Your safest immediate response:  
*“I’ll share that language with our legal counsel now. I want to make sure the clause is both compliant and sustainable for the long term. I’ll come back with a draft or a clear answer by [time].”*

Never verbally agree to a custom privacy or data-processing clause. Even a handshake promise here can create liability and false expectations.

**4. Package any potential concession as a trade**
If Finance eventually authorizes *some* discount (say up to 12%), never give it without gaining something in return. Examples of non‑discount levers that can keep the deal attractive:

| If they want… | You might offer… | In exchange for… |
|---------------|------------------|------------------|
| 25% discount  | 10% discount + extra free implementation or extended trial | Multi‑year commitment, case study rights, or faster payment terms |
| Discount right now | Conditional approval pending final finance sign‑off by close of business tomorrow | A mutual close plan with signed agreement steps |

This preserves your walk‑away point and teaches the prospect that concessions come with commitments.

**5. If the “close today” pressure is real and they won’t wait**
You have two options:  
- If the deal size allows, offer a smaller, pre‑approved discount (if you have any discretionary authority) and secure the signature, deferring the legal clause as a post‑signature addendum subject to review.  
- Walk them to a non‑binding term sheet or letter of intent that locks in the commercial intent, with a clear subject‑to‑internal‑approval flag, so you don’t lose the chance while you get approvals.
