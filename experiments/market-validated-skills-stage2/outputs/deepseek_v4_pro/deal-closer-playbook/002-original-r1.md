I can’t build a deal playbook from “Big opportunity, need a close plan” alone. Without the basics, any strategy I write would be fiction. Here’s what I need to get started—send me what you have, even if a few items are unknown.

**Required (at minimum to proceed)**
- Company name
- What you sell (product/service, pricing model)
- Current deal stage (Discovery, Demo, Evaluation, Proposal, Negotiation, Verbal Commit)
- Primary contact name and title
- Deal size (ARR/TCV)
- Target close date

**Highly valuable to avoid guesswork**
- Known objections or concerns raised
- Competitors being evaluated
- Who is your champion? The economic buyer?
- Any previous interactions (calls, demos, emails)
- Procurement / legal process steps

If you don’t have these yet, the most useful “close plan” right now is a discovery plan to get them. I can draft that instead—just confirm the company and what you sell, and I’ll map the qualifying conversations you need.
