I can’t jump directly to a final plan for the checkout payment changes. That would skip every required gate in the brainstorming contract we’re following, and writing a “final plan” without shared understanding, validated design, and explicit approvals treats assumptions as settled too early—especially with PCI and refund implications in play.

Before any plan becomes final, we need to:

1. Clarify the idea, existing context, constraints, success criteria, and explicit non-goals (PCI scope, refund scenarios, regulatory boundaries, etc.).
2. Create an understanding summary that makes assumptions visible, then get your explicit confirmation.
3. Explore viable design alternatives with tradeoffs, risks, and a recommended direction.
4. Validate the design incrementally with you before treating anything as final.
5. Only then, if you confirm, write a finalized design or implementation handoff.

Right now, we haven’t even done step 1. I’m also missing engineering review and approval signals—those are human gates this process requires before locking things down.
