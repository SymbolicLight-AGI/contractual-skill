# Renewal Risk Assessment & Rescue Handoff

## 1. Renewal Outlook
**High risk / churn likely** — The account shows a sharp erosion of health signals 45 days before renewal. The loss of the admin champion, a 3-point NPS drop, unresolved integration tickets, and a critical product gap (export scheduling) collectively indicate a strong probability of non-renewal or downsell. Current billing provides no offset to these risks; positive signals are minimal. Without immediate, coordinated intervention, this account will likely not renew.

## 3. Risk Drivers
- **Champion departure** — The known internal advocate is gone; renewal ownership and political cover are now absent.
- **NPS freefall (8 → 5)** — Suggests deep dissatisfaction likely tied to unresolved issues and missing features.
- **Unresolved integration tickets** — Operational pain; signals that the product isn’t delivering promised value.
- **Product gap (export scheduling)** — A required capability is missing, undermining the core use case.
- **Short time to renewal (45 days)** — Leaves little room to rebuild trust, fix integrations, and close feature gaps before the decision.

## 4. Opportunity Drivers
- **Billing is current** — No immediate financial friction; the account is still operational and paying, providing a window for rescue.
- **No explicit cancellation threat mentioned** — Still an active relationship, albeit deteriorating.
- **NPS not at floor** — 5 is poor but not unsalvageable if underlying issues are addressed quickly and the value narrative is rebuilt.

## 5. Recommended Internal Actions

**For Customer Success (CS)**
- Immediate owner: CS Manager.
- Conduct urgent executive alignment call: identify new champion, map decision-maker(s), and gauge sentiment.
- Deliver a “save plan” narrative: acknowledge the NPS drop, integration pain, and export gap; show a concrete timeline for fixes.
- Prepare a joint success plan with milestones before renewal date.
- **Gate:** All outbound messaging, meeting agendas, and promises require human approval before sending.

**For Support**
- Immediate owner: Support Lead.
- Escalate unresolved integration tickets to highest priority; assign dedicated engineer.
- Provide daily status updates to CS to maintain transparency with the customer.
- Root-cause analysis on integration failure; ensure not just resolution but trust rebuilding (e.g., post-mortem call).

**For Product**
- Immediate owner: Product Manager for relevant area.
- Confirm export scheduling gap severity and roadmap timeline (even a beta/private preview date).
- Evaluate a temporary workaround or manual service option to bridge until feature ships.
- Prepare a roadmap commitment letter (gated) signed by product leadership.
- **Gate:** Any roadmap commitment or timeline shared externally must pass through product management and leadership approval.

**For Finance**
- Immediate owner: Finance / Deal Desk.
- Model renewal scenarios (churn, downsell, flat renewal) and assess revenue exposure.
- Prepare flexible commercial options (if needed), but no discount or term changes authorized yet.
- Monitor billing status; ensure no surprise non-payment.
- **Gate:** Any pricing concession, extended terms, or commercial offer requires leadership approval via a deal review.

**For Leadership**
- Immediate owner: VP of Customer Success / CCO.
- Sponsor internal escalation: dedicate resources to this rescue without bureaucratic delay.
- Authorize emergency customer engagement (e.g., executive sponsor call) once CS has validated new champion.
- Signal willingness to consider short-term concessions (managed through approval gates).
- **Gate:** Any executive commitment (product delivery dates, SLA changes, financial concessions) must go through the defined approval process.

## 6. Human Gates & Handoffs Summary
- **CS** owns engagement plan; all customer-facing communication gated.
- **Support** resolves issues; no outbound promises without CS coordination.
- **Product** provides roadmap facts; external sharing gated.
- **Finance** prepares options; no discount/term changes without leadership.
- **Leadership** allocates resources and approves exceptions.

**Customer conversation themes (for CS to adapt after approval):**
1. “We heard you: the NPS feedback and integration issues are our top priority.”
2. “Here’s what we’ve fixed and what’s coming for export scheduling (timeline).”
3. “Let’s rebuild your success plan with your new team — you’re not starting over.”

- Actual usage data (logins, key workflows) to quantify adoption risk.
- Full support ticket history to understand scope of unresolved items.
- Executive sponsor relationship status.
