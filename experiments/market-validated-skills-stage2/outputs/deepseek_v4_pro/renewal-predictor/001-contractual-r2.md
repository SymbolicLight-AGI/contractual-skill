**Risk Drivers**
- **Usage decline (-18% WAU):** Suggests loss of stickiness, possible workflow dissatisfaction or unaddressed friction.
- **Support tickets rising:** May indicate product gaps, bugs, or inadequate enablement; escalating volume can erode confidence.
- **Executive sponsor departure:** The primary budget authority/champion at the economic-buyer level is gone, introducing re‑evaluation risk and potential delayed decision‑making.
- **Short time to renewal (75 days):** Compresses the window to stabilize and build a new executive relationship.

---

**Opportunity Drivers**
- **Admin champion engaged:** An internal advocate who can provide operational insight, influence users, and help navigate a new sponsor identification.
- **Two key workflows adopted:** Proof that the product is delivering value in at least two areas; can be used to anchor retention conversations and demonstrate ROI.
- **Moderate contract size ($120k ARR):** Large enough to justify dedicated save effort, but small enough that a customized recovery plan is feasible without extensive procurement friction.

**Recommended Internal Actions**
1. **Immediate discovery call with admin champion** to understand:
   - Root cause of WAU decline (role changes, usability, missing features, competitive activity)
   - Nature and resolution status of support tickets
   - Status of the executive sponsor replacement process
2. **Usage analytics deep dive** to isolate where drop-off occurs, comparing workflow adoption depth vs. breadth.
3. **Support ticket triage** – review open/closed tickets for common themes, severity, and response times; escalate unresolved technical issues.
4. **Stakeholder remapping** – identify potential new executive sponsor (e.g., admin’s manager, sponsor’s successor, a VP who benefits from the two adopted workflows).
5. **Internal health score note:** Label the account “red/high risk” in internal review queues and alert the assigned Customer Success Manager and Sales leader.
6. **Draft a value reinforcement brief** summarizing the two adopted workflows’ impact, ready to present once a new sponsor is engaged.
7. **Prepare a risk‑mitigation offer (if needed)** – e.g., free success‑services hours or a focused adoption sprint – but do **not** promise or price; that requires human approval.
