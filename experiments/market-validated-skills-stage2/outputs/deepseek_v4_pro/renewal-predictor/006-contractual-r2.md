# Renewal Rescue Handoff

**High risk of non-renewal or downsell.** The combination of a critical stakeholder departure, unresolved technical issues, a sharp NPS decline, and an unmet product need creates significant churn pressure within the final 45 days. Current billing standing is a positive signal but insufficient to offset the identified risks without urgent, coordinated intervention.

## 3. Risk Drivers
- **Stakeholder disruption:** Admin champion changed roles; no known replacement advocate.
- **Technical debt:** Unresolved integration tickets indicate ongoing friction and potential operational impact.
- **Sentiment decay:** NPS drop from 8 (promoter) to 5 (passive/detractor) signals declining loyalty and possible negative word-of-mouth.
- **Product gap:** Export scheduling is an explicit unmet need, potentially a competitive exit driver.
- **Time pressure:** 45 days leaves minimal margin for full relationship recovery and technical resolution.

## 4. Opportunity Drivers
- **Billing current:** Customer is current on payments, suggesting ongoing commercial relationship and no immediate financial exit signals.
- **Engagement window:** 45 days provides a narrow but real opportunity to demonstrate commitment through issue resolution and executive engagement.
- **No formal cancellation notice:** No termination signal is mentioned; the door remains open for retention if swift action is taken.

## 5. Recommended Internal Actions

### Customer Success (CS)
- **Immediate outreach (human-gated):** Identify new admin contact and map the post-champion influencer network. Schedule a listening session to capture current pain points.
- **Rebuild advocacy:** Propose a structured engagement plan (executive business review, usage review) to re-establish trust and uncover latent value.
- **Sentiment triage:** Directly address the NPS decline; ask for specific feedback and demonstrate action.

### Support
- **Prioritize integration tickets:** Escalate unresolved integration tickets to top-priority resolution with a transparent timeline communicated back to the customer.
- **Root cause analysis:** Determine if the unresolved tickets reflect a systemic gap or capacity issue; brief CS on realistic remediation timeline.

### Product
- **Export scheduling gap:** Provide a concrete roadmap response (e.g., committed timeline, private beta access, or workaround options) to address the stated product gap.
- **Voice of customer:** Feed the unmet need and churn risk into product prioritization; quantify revenue at risk.

### Finance
- **Monitor billing health:** Current standing is green, but prepare for potential late payment, renegotiation, or last-minute discount requests. Flag the account for receivable watch.
- **No immediate billing action needed.**

### Leadership
- **Risk visibility:** Brief account executive leadership on the high-risk account and potential revenue impact. Prepare for possible executive-to-executive outreach (human-gated) to signal commitment and salvage the relationship.

## 6. Human Gates and Handoffs
- **Customer-facing outreach** (CS calls, emails, meeting requests) must be approved by CS lead before execution.
- **Any pricing, discount, or contract term changes** require sales/finance leadership approval.
- **Executive escalation or stakeholder mapping outreach** must be cleared by leadership.
- **CRM updates, churn forecasting external to the team, and cancellation probability statements** are gated and should not be recorded as facts.
- **Technical resolution commitments** from support and product must be internally validated before sharing with the customer.

The situation calls for a tightly coordinated rescue sprint across CS, support, product, and leadership, with strict adherence to human-gated customer communications.
