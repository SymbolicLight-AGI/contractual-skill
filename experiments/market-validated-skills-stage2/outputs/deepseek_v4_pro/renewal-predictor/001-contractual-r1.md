**3. Risk drivers**  
- **Executive sponsor departed**: The person who likely held budget authority and strategic alignment is gone, creating a relationship vacuum and potential loss of internal advocacy at the decision-maker level.  
- **Weekly active users declining by 18%**: A material drop suggests waning habitual usage, possible workflow abandonment, or the start of shadow-IT/substitution behaviour.  
- **Support tickets up**: Rising support volume often correlates with frustration, unresolved issues, or product instability – any of which can erode renewal confidence.  
- **Compressed timeline (75 days)**: There is limited time to stabilize the account and demonstrate renewed value, especially with a missing executive sponsor.

**4. Opportunity drivers**  
- **Admin champion remains engaged**: This person can provide ground-truth on the root causes of the slowdown, guide access to other stakeholders, and co-create a recovery plan.  
- **Two key workflows adopted**: Workflow adoption signals that some value is being realised; these can serve as proof points to rebuild the business case and expand from.  
- **Recoverable ARR ($120k)**: The contract size justifies dedicated effort; the cost of churn likely outweighs the cost of targeted rescue actions.

**5. Recommended internal actions**

- **Immediate triage (next 3 business days)**  
  - Review all recent support tickets to categorise themes (bug, configuration, training, feature request).  
  - Schedule a “health-check” call with the admin champion to listen, not sell – understand the 18% drop, discover how the sponsor departure is landing, and identify emerging internal supporters.  
  - Map the account’s original value drivers against current usage data; pinpoint which workflows have declined.

- **Short-term (within 2 weeks)**  
  - Build a joint success plan with the admin champion that ties the two adopted workflows to a measurable business outcome the executive team cares about.  
  - Develop a warm introduction path to a potential new executive sponsor (e.g., via the admin champion’s manager or by bringing in a customer success executive for a peer conversation).  
  - Outline a “value review” deck that contrasts initial objectives with current state, and shows a 60-day path to recovery.

- **Ongoing until renewal**  
  - Establish a cadence of bi‑weekly check-ins with the champion and, once identified, the new sponsor.  
  - Run a support-deflection or enablement session to address the ticket-creating root causes.  
  - Document all engagement signals in a simple health-score card (active user recovery, ticket volume trend, champion sentiment) for weekly internal review.
