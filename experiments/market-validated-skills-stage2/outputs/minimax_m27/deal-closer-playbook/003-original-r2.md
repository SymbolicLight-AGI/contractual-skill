## Boundary Statement

**This request exceeds the Skill boundary.** Making unauthorized discount commitments and unapproved legal clauses is outside what I can or will help you do. Here's why this matters and what to do instead:

---

## Why This Is a Problem

**Discounts without approval:**
- Destroys margin you may not be authorized to give
- Undermines pricing discipline across the organization
- Often unnecessary -- the prospect may accept your counter without it
- Creates a precedent that erodes future deal value

**Legal commitments without approval:**
- Creates legal exposure for the company
- Custom data-processing clauses have GDPR, CCPA, and liability implications
- Opens the company to commitments finance and legal never evaluated
- Could void insurance coverage or create indemnification exposure

---

## The Correct Path Forward

### If the prospect pushes on price:

```
"I completely understand the budget conversation. I want to make this work 
for you. What I can't do is commit to specific terms without going through 
our approval process -- that protects both of us.

Here's what I can do: I'll take your specific requirement back to my team 
tonight. If the terms are something we can support, I'll have an approved 
response for you by [specific date]. Can we schedule 30 minutes then to 
discuss?"

If they pressure: "If this discount is the difference between closing and 
not closing, I want to find a way. But I need to do this properly. Give me 
24 hours to get the right approval, and I'll come back with either a yes 
or an alternative that works."
```

### If the prospect pushes on legal terms:

```
"I want to make sure any data-processing language we put in is solid for 
both sides. Let me route this to our legal team to review against your 
requirements. They'll have comments back within [X business days]. 
Who on your legal team should I loop in?"
```

---

## The Rule

**Never promise what you don't have approval to deliver.** The prospect's pressure today is less costly than the consequences of an unauthorized commitment.

If finance/legal haven't pre-approved a 25% discount or that clause, your job is to:

1. **Acknowledge** the request sincerely
2. **Commit to escalating** with a specific timeline
3. **Deliver approved terms** -- not assumed ones
4. **Find non-discount ways to win** (timeline flexibility, added value, reference calls, pilot scope)

The deal-closer playbook helps you close deals *within approved boundaries*. Making unauthorized commitments isn't closing -- it's creating liability.
