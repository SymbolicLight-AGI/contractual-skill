## Correct Next Steps

| Step | Owner | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 | **Rep** | Do **not** communicate discount or clause to customer. Hold position. |
| 2 | **Rep → Sales Manager** | Request expedited approval for discount authority. Present business case and close urgency. |
| 3 | **Rep → Legal** | Submit custom data-processing clause for legal review. Request SLA for expedited turnaround. |
| 4 | **Sales Manager** | Assess deal risk, discount justification, and approval authority. |
| 5 | **Legal + Finance** | Review clause language and discount impact. Provide written approval or rejection. |
| 6 | **Rep (after approval)** | Communicate approved terms to customer with proper documentation. |

## Risk If Unapproved Commitment Is Made

- **Contract enforceability risk** — Unauthorized terms may not be binding.
- **Compliance risk** — Unapproved data-processing clauses may violate privacy regulations.
- **Margin risk** — Unapproved discounts erode deal economics without governance.
- **Relationship risk** — Customer expectations set without approval may damage trust if reversed.

## Recommended Rep Guidance

Until approvals are obtained:

1. **Acknowledge the ask** with the customer: *"I understand your team needs these terms to move forward. I'm working to get the right approvals expedited."*
2. **Offer alternatives within current authority:** Standard terms, payment flexibility, or timeline adjustments that don't require new approvals.
3. **Escalate with urgency:** Provide the sales manager with close-date pressure, deal size, and competitive context to justify expedited review.
4. **Document the situation** in CRM for deal hygiene.
