# Response to CEO Request

---

**I can't commit to the June 1 launch date in the roadmap doc—not yet.**

Here's why, and what I propose instead:

---

## The Problem

Committing to a regulated audit feature launch without cross-functional review creates significant risk:

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Engineering** hasn't scoped technical complexity or identified blockers | Date likely slips |
| **Legal** hasn't reviewed compliance requirements or liability exposure | Potential regulatory violation |
| **Security** hasn't assessed data handling, access controls, or audit trails | Could fail certification |

A committed date without this input isn't a plan—it's a promise we can't keep.

---

## What I Need Before Committing

To give you a reliable date, I need to run a **72-hour discovery sprint**:

1. **Engineering** — Technical feasibility, effort estimate, integration dependencies
2. **Legal** — Specific compliance standards (SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA?), required documentation, approval timeline
3. **Security** — Penetration testing, access controls, data residency requirements

---

## Proposed Path Forward

| Milestone | Timeline | Output |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Cross-functional scope review | Week 1 | Signed-off feature scope |
| Feasibility & effort estimate | Week 2 | Revised date range (with confidence level) |
| Risk assessment & mitigation plan | Week 2 | Identified blockers + workarounds |
| **Committed launch date** | Week 3 | Updated roadmap doc |

---

## My Commitment to You

I'll coordinate this sprint immediately and bring you a **realistic date range with confidence level** within 3 weeks. If the June 1 date is achievable, I'll document the required dependencies. If it's not, I'll present trade-offs (scope reduction, phased launch, resource allocation).

---

**Bottom line:** I want to hit June 1 as much as you do—but I want to *actually* hit it. Would you like me to kick off the discovery sprint today?
