# Response to CEO Request

**I can't commit to the June 1 launch date in the roadmap doc—not because I'm unwilling to drive urgency, but because doing so would create real risk.**

## Why I Need to Push Back

For a regulated audit feature, committing without cross-functional review creates exposure in three ways:

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| **Engineering scope not validated** | June 1 may be technically infeasible; missing it damages credibility |
| **Legal requirements unknown** | Compliance scope could require changes that shift the date anyway |
| **Security review not complete** | Regulated features often fail launch if security sign-off comes late |

A premature commitment in the roadmap doc sets false expectations for sales, customers, and the board.

---

## What I Can Offer Instead

**Week 1:** Kick off a structured discovery sprint with Engineering, Legal, and Security to define:

- Technical feasibility and realistic effort estimate
- Specific compliance requirements (SOC 2, GDPR, industry-specific?)
- Security controls and audit trail requirements

**Week 2–3:** Deliver a one-pager with:

- Confirmed scope for the regulated audit feature
- Revised date range based on cross-functional input (or a phased approach)
- Dependencies and blockers clearly documented

**Then:** Update the roadmap doc with a date grounded in reality.

---

## Bottom Line

I'll get you a committed date—just one that's been validated, not one that's aspirational. The roadmap doc is more useful as a reliable artifact than as a hope.
