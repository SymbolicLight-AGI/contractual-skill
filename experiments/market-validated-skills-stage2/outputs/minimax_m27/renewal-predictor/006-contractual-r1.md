## 2. Risk Drivers

1. **Stakeholder Gap** — The admin champion has changed roles. Depending on whether this person left the account or took a new internal role, there may be no active internal champion driving adoption or renewal advocacy.

2. **Satisfaction Decline** — The NPS drop from 8 to 5 indicates meaningful customer dissatisfaction. This change, combined with known blockers, suggests the customer is actively weighing the value of the relationship.

3. **Unresolved Blockers** — Integration tickets remaining open after an unknown duration signals operational friction. If these are blocking core workflows, the customer may be losing confidence in the product.

4. **Feature Gap** — The export scheduling gap is confirmed. Without knowing the severity or frequency of use, this represents a known deficiency that the customer may be using to justify non-renewal.

5. **Timing Risk** — 45 days provides a narrow window to address stakeholder loss, technical blockers, and satisfaction recovery before renewal discussions occur.

---

## 3. Opportunity Drivers

1. **Billing Compliance** — Current billing status suggests the account is financially healthy and engaged. The customer is not in arrears, which indicates ongoing operational use.

2. **Champion Available** — If the admin champion moved to a new role internally rather than leaving the company, there may be an opportunity to reconnect through a new contact and re-establish advocacy.

3. **NPS Still Positive** — An NPS of 5, while dropped, is not negative. The customer has not expressed dissatisfaction to the point of active detraction. There is a recovery window.

4. **Product Gap Known** — Unlike hidden frustrations, the export scheduling gap is identified. Addressing it or providing a timeline could restore confidence.

5. **Rescue Window** — 45 days is sufficient to conduct targeted outreach, resolve blockers, and prepare a value-focused renewal conversation—if coordinated quickly.
