## 4. Objection Playbook

### Anticipated Early-Stage Objections

**OBJECTION: "We are just exploring right now / Not ready to commit."**

WHY THEY SAY THIS:
Low urgency, no quantified pain, or internal alignment missing.

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
1. Acknowledge: "Totally fair. Most teams start by exploring options before committing."
2. Reframe: "Exploration is exactly why I want to map your success criteria now, so you have a clear benchmark when you're ready to move."
3. Evidence: "Teams that define their evaluation criteria upfront cut their decision cycle by 40%."
4. Confirm: "Would it make sense to spend 20 minutes next week mapping what a successful evaluation looks like for your team?"

EXAMPLE RESPONSE:
"I completely understand. Since you're in exploration mode, I'd rather help you build a clear evaluation framework now so you're not starting from scratch when budget opens up. Can we block 20 minutes to map your must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?"

IF THEY PUSH BACK:
Offer a lightweight self-assessment or ROI calculator they can use internally. Keep the door open with a 30-day check-in.

**OBJECTION: "I need to check with my team / boss."**

WHY THEY SAY THIS:
Single-threaded contact, unclear authority, or internal consensus not built.

RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
1. Acknowledge: "Makes sense. You shouldn't be the only one carrying this."
2. Reframe: "Let's make sure you have everything you need to present this internally without getting grilled."
3. Evidence: "I'll build a 1-page business case you can forward. It covers cost, timeline, and expected ROI."
4. Confirm: "Who else should be in the room for our next call so we can answer their questions directly?"

EXAMPLE RESPONSE:
"Absolutely. To save you time, I'll draft a 1-page summary you can share with your manager. Who else should we loop in so we can answer their questions in one meeting instead of playing email tag?"

IF THEY PUSH BACK:
Ask for a direct intro to the decision-maker. "Would you be open to a quick 3-way intro so I can take the heavy lifting off your plate?"

## 5. Competitive Positioning

COMPETITOR: Status Quo / Inaction / Manual Workarounds

THEIR LIKELY PITCH:
"We can handle this with spreadsheets, existing tools, or by delaying the purchase until next budget cycle."

WHERE THEY WIN:
Zero upfront cost, no implementation friction, familiar to current team.

WHERE WE WIN:
Scalability, reduced error rates, time savings, compliance/security, and measurable ROI that compounds over time.

LANDMINE QUESTIONS:
1. "How many hours per week does your team currently spend on manual workarounds for this process?"
2. "What happens to your SLAs or customer experience if this process breaks during peak season?"
3. "If you delay this by 6 months, what is the estimated cost of lost productivity or missed revenue?"

IF THE PROSPECT FAVORS THE COMPETITOR:
Do not attack inaction. Quantify the hidden cost of doing nothing. Build a phased rollout plan to reduce perceived risk.

TRAP TO AVOID:
Assuming they will buy just because the product is better. Inaction wins when the pain isn't quantified.
