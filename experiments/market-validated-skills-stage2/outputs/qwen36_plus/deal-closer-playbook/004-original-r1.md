## 4. Objection Playbook

### Known Objection Responses

**OBJECTION:** "We need to complete the security questionnaire and vendor onboarding before moving forward."

**WHY THEY SAY THIS:**
Standard enterprise risk mitigation. They cannot legally or procedurally sign until compliance and vendor management clear the deal.

**RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:**
1. Acknowledge: "Completely understand. Security and procurement are critical to protecting your data and ensuring a smooth rollout."
2. Reframe: "We've already prepped the security packet and onboarding forms so we can run this in parallel with your timeline review."
3. Evidence: "We've cleared this exact process with [Similar Company] in under 10 business days by aligning our security team directly with yours."
4. Confirm: "If I get the completed questionnaire and onboarding packet to your security/procurement leads by [Date], does that keep us on track for your target go-live?"

**EXAMPLE RESPONSE:**
"I know security and vendor onboarding are mandatory steps on your end. I've already queued our security team to complete your questionnaire within 48 hours, and I'm submitting the vendor onboarding packet today. I'll also attach the implementation timeline you requested so your team can see exactly how we'll roll this out without disrupting current workflows. If we get these three items moving in parallel, we can clear the compliance hurdles and stay on track for your target date. Does that align with your internal process?"

**IF THEY PUSH BACK:**
Offer a direct intro between your security lead and theirs. Provide a pre-filled vendor onboarding template. Ask: "What specific section of the questionnaire or onboarding form typically causes the longest delay? I'll have our team pre-address it."

### Anticipated Objections

- **Timing/Implementation**: "We don't have bandwidth to implement right now." -> Provide phased rollout, dedicated CSM, and zero-downtime migration plan.
- **Authority**: "I need to check with my boss/legal." -> Supply champion with a 1-page executive summary + ROI model.
- **Risk**: "What if the rollout fails?" -> Share reference call, implementation SLA, and rollback guarantee.
