## 4. Objection Playbook

### Anticipated Early-Stage Objections

**OBJECTION: "We are just exploring options right now."**
WHY THEY SAY THIS: Low urgency, no budget allocated, or they are in information-gathering mode.
RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
1. Acknowledge: "Totally fair. Most teams start by mapping the landscape before committing."
2. Reframe: "Exploration is smart, but it's most effective when tied to a specific metric you're trying to move."
3. Evidence: "Teams that define success criteria early cut evaluation time by 40%."
4. Confirm: "What's the one metric you'd want to improve if you moved forward?"
EXAMPLE RESPONSE: "I get that. To make sure I don't waste your time, what specific outcome would make this worth a deeper look? If we can map a path to that, we can keep this lean."
IF THEY PUSH BACK: Offer a 15-minute diagnostic call instead of a full demo.

**OBJECTION: "I need to check with my team/boss first."**
WHY THEY SAY THIS: Lack of authority, internal alignment missing, or polite deflection.
RESPONSE FRAMEWORK:
1. Acknowledge: "Makes sense. You shouldn't move forward without alignment."
2. Reframe: "Let's build a quick one-pager you can share internally so you don't have to field questions alone."
3. Evidence: "We've helped reps like you get quick internal buy-in by focusing on [specific ROI metric]."
4. Confirm: "Who else needs to see this, and what's their biggest concern?"
EXAMPLE RESPONSE: "Happy to wait. To save you time, what's the one thing your manager will ask about first? I'll draft a 3-bullet summary you can forward."
IF THEY PUSH BACK: Ask for a joint intro call with the decision-maker.

## 5. Competitive Positioning

COMPETITOR: Status Quo / Inaction / Manual Workarounds

THEIR LIKELY PITCH: "We can handle this with spreadsheets, existing tools, or by doing nothing for now."
WHERE THEY WIN: Zero upfront cost, familiar workflow, no implementation risk.
WHERE WE WIN: Scalability, reduced manual error, faster time-to-value, measurable ROI.
LANDMINE QUESTIONS:
1. "How many hours per week does your team currently spend on [manual process]?"
2. "What happens to that workflow when volume doubles or headcount changes?"
3. "If you keep doing this manually for 12 months, what's the hidden cost in errors or missed opportunities?"
IF THE PROSPECT FAVORS THE COMPETITOR: Quantify the cost of inaction. Build a lightweight ROI calculator. Do not push for a close; push for a pilot or diagnostic.
TRAP TO AVOID: Attacking their current process. Frame it as "outgrowing" rather than "broken."
